Roll Up Mosquito Screen – Space‑Saving, Retractable Protection for Any Window or Door

Published on May 3, 2026 | By MBORMC Team

A roll up mosquito screen (also called a retractable screen) is one of the most convenient insect protection solutions available. It consists of a mesh that rolls up into a slim, discreet cassette when not in use, and pulls down or across when protection is needed. MBORMC manufactures custom roll up screens for windows, doors, and even large openings. In this guide, we’ll explain how roll up screens work, their advantages over fixed screens, and how you can install one yourself – no professional required.

What Is a Roll Up Mosquito Screen?

A roll up mosquito screen uses a spring‑loaded roller mechanism inside a compact cassette. The cassette can be mounted horizontally (above a window or door) or vertically (at the side). When you need insect protection, you pull the screen down (or across) using a handle and it locks into place. When not needed, you release the latch and the screen automatically retracts into the cassette. The cassette is typically only 2 inches (5 cm) wide and can be color‑matched to your window or door frame.

Why Choose a Roll Up Screen?

  • Unobstructed view: When retracted, the screen disappears completely, leaving your window or door clear.
  • Space‑saving: No bulky fixed frame – perfect for small spaces and apartments.
  • Aesthetic: The cassette blends with the frame, and there’s no visible screen when not in use.
  • Easy to use: Pull down with one hand – smooth spring action.
  • Ideal for renters: Minimal drilling required (screw holes can be filled).

Types of Roll Up Screens from MBORMC

  • Top‑mount (vertical roll‑down): Cassette mounted above the window, pulls down. Most common for windows and single doors.
  • Side‑mount (horizontal roll‑across): Cassette mounted at the side, pulls across. Ideal for sliding doors and wide windows.
  • Reverse roll (for outward‑opening windows): Cassette mounts on the inside, but the screen rolls up from the bottom. Custom engineering available.

Mesh Material Options

We offer roll up screens with PET fiber mesh (lightweight, UV‑resistant, 18×16 density) – standard for residential use. For high‑traffic areas or homes with pets, we recommend 304 stainless steel mesh for extra durability. However, note that stainless steel mesh is heavier and may require a stronger spring. Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black. Dark mesh reduces glare and is less visible from the outside.

DIY Installation – Step by Step

Installing a roll up mosquito screen is a simple DIY project. Steps may vary slightly depending on whether it’s top‑mount or side‑mount. Here’s the general process:

  • Measure your window or door opening width and height.
  • Order the custom roll up screen from MBORMC with your exact dimensions and cassette orientation.
  • Mount the cassette using screws (wall anchors provided). Use a level to ensure it’s straight.
  • For top‑mount: attach the side guide tracks (if included) or simply let the screen hang with magnetic side seals.
  • For side‑mount: attach the top and bottom guide rails.
  • Pull the screen to the opposite side and install the latch.
  • Test retraction – it should roll up smoothly.

Basic tools required: drill, screwdriver, level, measuring tape. Total time: 20–40 minutes.

Custom Sizing – Perfect for Any Opening

Unlike store‑bought roll up screens that come in fixed sizes, MBORMC manufactures each screen to your exact measurements. You provide width and height (in cm or inches). We cut the cassette, mesh, and rails precisely. This ensures a gap‑free fit, critical for keeping out small insects. Maximum width for a single cassette is 1.5m; for wider openings, consider a bi‑parting system (two cassettes) or a pleated screen.

Where to Use Roll Up Screens

  • Kitchen windows: Roll up when cooking, deploy when you want fresh air without flies.
  • Bedroom windows: Keep mosquitoes out while sleeping – retract during the day for a clear view.
  • Patio sliding doors: Side‑mount roll up screens are very popular.
  • Balcony doors: Perfect for apartments where space is tight.
  • RVs and campers: Custom sizes for vehicle windows.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does the spring mechanism last?
A: Our springs are rated for over 10,000 cycles – equivalent to daily use for 27 years.

Q: Can I install a roll up screen on a window that opens outward?
A: Yes, but careful measurement is needed. For outward‑opening windows, we may recommend a side‑mount cassette or a reverse roll system. Contact us for guidance.

Q: How do I clean the screen?
A: Vacuum the mesh with a brush attachment. For deeper cleaning, you can pull the screen out and wipe with a damp cloth. Do not machine wash.

Q: Is the screen secure against pets?
A: PET mesh can be scratched. Use stainless steel mesh for pet‑proof protection, but note that the heavier mesh may affect spring tension.
