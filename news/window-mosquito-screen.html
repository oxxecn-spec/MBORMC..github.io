Window Mosquito Screen – Tailored Protection for Every Window Type

Published on May 1, 2026 | By MBORMC Team
Window Mosquito Screen Mosquito Screen Insect Screen Retractable Screen Velcro Screen

Windows are the primary entry points for mosquitoes, flies, and other flying insects. A well‑fitted window mosquito screen allows you to enjoy fresh air without inviting pests into your home. MBORMC manufactures custom window screens for all window types – sliding, casement, awning, sash, tilt‑and‑turn, and even arched windows. In this guide, we’ll help you choose the right screen style for your window and explain the DIY installation process.

Why Install a Window Mosquito Screen?

Open windows are essential for ventilation, especially during warm weather. But an open window without a screen is an invitation to mosquitoes, flies, wasps, and other insects. Window screens provide a physical barrier that stops insects while letting in light and air. Unlike chemical sprays or electronic devices, screens work 24/7 without any effort or ongoing cost. They also keep out dust, pollen, and small debris, improving indoor air quality.

Types of Window Mosquito Screens from MBORMC

We offer several screen styles to match different window configurations:

  • Retractable Roll‑Up Window Screens: The mesh rolls into a compact cassette mounted at the top or side of the window. When not in use, the screen is completely hidden. Ideal for windows that you don’t always need screened, like those only opened occasionally.
  • Fixed Frame Screens: A permanent screen with an aluminum frame that attaches to the window frame via screws or clips. This is the most common type for windows that are opened frequently. It provides a secure, tight fit.
  • Self‑Adhesive Velcro Screens: A no‑drill, tool‑free solution. The Velcro tape sticks to the window frame, and the screen presses onto it. Perfect for renters or temporary installations. Easy to remove in winter.
  • Magnetic Window Screens: Similar to Velcro but uses magnetic strips. The screen snaps onto a magnetic frame attached to the window. Great for metal window frames.
  • Pleated/Folding Window Screens: A folding mesh that expands horizontally or vertically. Can be used for wider windows or where a roll‑up cassette is not suitable.

Compatibility with Different Window Types

  • Sliding Windows: Use a vertical retractable screen that mounts at the side, or a fixed frame that covers the entire opening. The screen must allow the moving sash to slide past.
  • Casement Windows (crank out): A fixed frame screen that mounts on the interior side works best. The screen must be placed where it doesn’t interfere with the crank mechanism. Roll‑up screens can also be mounted.
  • Awning Windows (hinged at top): A fixed frame screen on the inside is typical. Ensure the screen is low‑profile so it doesn’t block the opening arc.
  • Double‑Hung / Sash Windows: A fixed frame screen that covers the entire window or separate screens for each sash. Retractable screens can also be mounted at the top of the lower sash.
  • Tilt‑and‑Turn Windows: A fixed frame screen mounted on the interior side works well. The screen should be positioned to allow the window to tilt inward without hitting.
  • Arched or Round Windows: MBORMC can manufacture custom‑shaped frames to match any non‑rectangular window. Please provide a template or detailed drawing.

Material Options: PET vs. 304 Stainless Steel

MBORMC uses two primary mesh materials for window screens. PET fiber mesh is lightweight, breathable, and affordable. It provides excellent insect protection while maintaining high transparency (over 85% openness). PET is UV‑stabilized and won’t rust. It’s available in grey, dark grey, or black to match your window frame. 304 stainless steel mesh is incredibly strong, pet‑proof, and fire‑resistant. It’s ideal for ground‑floor windows or areas where pets may scratch. Stainless steel offers a more premium look and longer lifespan (10–20 years).

DIY Installation – No Professional Needed

All MBORMC window mosquito screens are designed for hassle‑free DIY installation. Each order includes a step‑by‑step guide with pictures. For adhesive‑mount screens, you simply clean the frame, peel the tape, and press. For fixed frame screens, you’ll need a screwdriver to attach the brackets or clips. For retractable screens, you may need a drill to mount the cassette, but we provide all hardware and templates. The entire process takes 15–30 minutes per window. Detailed video tutorials are available upon request.

Frequently Asked Questions About Window Mosquito Screens

Q: Can I install a window screen on a window that opens outward?
A: Yes. For outward‑opening casement or awning windows, we recommend an interior‑mounted fixed frame screen or a retractable screen that doesn’t interfere with the opening mechanism.

Q: Will a window screen block my view?
A: Our high‑transparency PET mesh has over 85% openness. You’ll barely notice it from inside. The grey/black mesh also reduces glare and improves outward visibility compared to white mesh.

Q: How do I clean my window screen?
A: Use a soft brush or vacuum with a brush attachment. For deeper cleaning, remove the screen (Velcro or clip systems) and rinse with mild soap and water. Retractable screens can be cleaned while in place using a damp cloth.

Q: Do you offer screens for very large picture windows?
A: Yes. For fixed picture windows that never open, you may not need a screen. But if you want to open them, we can provide extra‑wide retractable or folding screens. Contact us for a custom quote.
