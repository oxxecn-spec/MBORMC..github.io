Mosquito Screen for French Door – Elegant Protection for Double Doors

Published on May 5, 2026 | By MBORMC Team

French doors bring timeless elegance and abundant natural light to any home. But their double‑door design makes them challenging to protect with conventional single‑panel screens. A mosquito screen for French door requires a solution that can cover the entire width while allowing each door to open independently. MBORMC offers custom bi‑parting folding (pleated) screens and retractable screens specifically designed for French doors. In this guide, we’ll help you choose the best screen style, explain installation, and provide measurement tips.

Why French Doors Need a Specialized Screen

Standard sliding screens are designed for single openings; they won’t work on French doors that swing outward or inward. A single retractable screen covering both doors would block the ability to open just one leaf. The ideal solution is either a bi‑parting folding screen (two screens meeting in the middle) or two separate retractable screens (one per door). A properly fitted screen allows you to:

  • Open one door for ventilation while keeping the other screened.
  • Open both doors fully for a wide, unobstructed passage.
  • Enjoy fresh air without letting in mosquitoes, flies, or other insects.

Best Screen Types for French Doors

  • Bi‑Parting Folding (Pleated) Screen – The most popular solution. Two accordion‑fold screens stack on the left and right sides. They glide on top and bottom tracks and meet in the middle with a magnetic or mechanical latch. Each screen can be opened independently, allowing you to use one door at a time.
  • Two Separate Retractable Screens – If you have limited side space, you can install a retractable screen on each door leaf. Each screen rolls into a slim cassette at the side. When not needed, both screens disappear. This is more expensive but offers an invisible look.
  • Magnetic Mesh Curtain (for renters) – A single wide curtain that splits vertically with magnets. It covers both doors but you must open the entire curtain to walk through. Not as durable, but no‑drill and cheap.

How Bi‑Parting Folding Screens Work

A bi‑parting folding screen consists of two pleated mesh panels, each with a top rail and bottom track (or tension wire). The panels stack at the left and right sides when open. When closed, they meet in the middle. A handle on each panel allows you to slide it open. The mesh is made of PET or stainless steel, and the frame is aluminum. This system works for French doors that open outward (the tracks are inside) or inward (tracks may need to be surface‑mounted).

Mesh Material Options

For French doors, PET fiber mesh (18×16) is usually sufficient – it’s lightweight, UV‑resistant, and offers excellent visibility. If you have pets or high traffic, choose 304 stainless steel mesh. Stainless steel pleated mesh is stiffer and more durable, though slightly heavier. Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black. Dark mesh reduces glare and is less visible from outside.

How to Measure for a French Door Screen

  • Measure the total width of both doors when closed (from left jamb to right jamb).
  • Measure the height from the top of the door frame to the floor (or to the bottom track location).
  • For bi‑parting screens, we will manufacture two panels that each cover half the width (plus an overlap).
  • If your French doors open inward, ensure there is enough clearance for the tracks (usually 10‑15mm).

DIY Installation – Weekend Project

Installing a bi‑parting folding screen is more involved than a single screen but still achievable for a DIYer. Steps:

  1. Install the top rail using screws (wall anchors provided). Use a level to ensure it’s straight.
  2. Install the bottom track aligned with the top rail.
  3. Attach the left and right screen assemblies to the rail – they clip into place.
  4. Install the handles and center latch.
  5. Test sliding – adjust the tension if needed.

Basic tools required: drill, screwdriver, level, measuring tape. Time: 1‑2 hours for two panels.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I install a folding screen on French doors that open inward?
A: Yes, but the tracks will be surface‑mounted on the interior side. Ensure the doors don’t hit the tracks when fully open (measure clearance).

Q: How durable is the pleated mesh?
A: PET pleated mesh lasts 5‑8 years; stainless steel lasts 10‑20 years. The pleats may wear, but replacement mesh can be ordered.

Q: Can I open just one door at a time?
A: Yes, with a bi‑parting screen, each panel can be opened independently. Slide the left panel to the left to access the left door, etc.

Q: Do you offer motorized folding screens for French doors?
A: Yes, motorized options are available for very wide or heavy screens. Contact us for details.
