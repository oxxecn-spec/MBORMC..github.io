Mosquito Screen for Patio – Create a Bug‑Free Outdoor Living Space

A patio is an extension of your home – a place for dining, relaxing, and entertaining. But mosquitoes, flies, and other insects can quickly ruin the experience. A mosquito screen for patio allows you to enjoy your outdoor space without the buzz and bites. MBORMC offers custom screens for patios of all sizes – from small balconies to large covered patios and screened porches. In this guide, we’ll explore the best screen solutions for patios, how to choose weather‑resistant materials, and installation options that blend seamlessly with your home.

Why Your Patio Needs Mosquito Screens

Patios are typically semi‑enclosed or open to the outdoors, making them prime targets for insects. Even if you have a covered roof, mosquitoes can fly in from the sides. A screen allows you to:

  • Enjoy fresh air without swatting insects.
  • Protect food and drinks from flies during outdoor meals.
  • Create a safe play area for children and pets without insect bites.
  • Reduce the need for chemical repellents or citronella candles.
  • Increase your home’s usable living space, especially during summer evenings.

Best Screen Types for Patios

The ideal screen depends on your patio’s structure and how you use the space.

  • Retractable Screens for Patio Doors – For sliding or hinged doors leading to the patio, retractable screens roll into a slim cassette and disappear when not in use. Perfect for preserving your view.
  • Sliding Screen Doors – For sliding glass patio doors, a traditional sliding screen is durable and classic. Available with pet‑proof stainless steel mesh.
  • Folding (Pleated) Screens for Wide Openings – If your patio has a large opening (e.g., a wide archway or a wall of glass doors), a folding screen can cover spans up to 4 meters.
  • Fixed Frame Screens for Screened Porches – To convert an existing porch into a fully screened room, we supply rigid frame screens that attach to the structural posts. Custom sizes and colours available.
  • Magnetic Curtains for Temporary Patio Enclosures – For renters or seasonal use, magnetic mesh curtains can be hung on a Patio door or opening. No drilling, easily removable.

Mesh Material for Patio Screens

Patio screens are exposed to sun, rain, and wind. We recommend:

  • 304 stainless steel mesh – Extremely durable, rust‑proof, and pet‑resistant. Ideal for patios that get heavy use or are near gardens. Lasts 10‑20 years.
  • PET fiber mesh – Lighter, more affordable, and offers excellent visibility (85% openness). Suitable for covered patios or light use. Lasts 5‑10 years.

Dark grey or black mesh is recommended – it reduces glare and is less visible from outside, preserving the open feel of the patio.

Measuring for Patio Screens

  • For patio doors: measure the door opening width and height as you would for a standard door.
  • For open arches or large openings: measure the width between posts or walls and the height from floor to beam/ceiling.
  • If you plan to screen an entire porch, measure each individual section between support columns.
  • Take multiple measurements and use the smallest dimensions.

DIY vs. Professional Installation

For standard patio doors, DIY installation is straightforward. For large openings or whole‑porch enclosures, we recommend professional installation. We can refer local contractors. All screens come with detailed instructions and hardware. For DIYers, basic tools (drill, screwdriver, level) are required.

Maintaining Your Patio Screens

  • Clean the mesh regularly with a soft brush or vacuum. For stainless steel, you can use a garden hose.
  • Inspect tracks and rollers for debris; clean annually.
  • For retractable screens, avoid forcing the screen if it jams – check for obstructions in the guide rails.
  • Replace damaged mesh promptly – we supply spare mesh and spline.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I screen an open patio with no ceiling?
A: Yes, but you’ll need a top rail or cable support. We can design freestanding screens that mount to the ground and side walls.

Q: Will a patio screen block the breeze?
A: No. Our mesh has high openness (75‑85%), allowing plenty of airflow while blocking insects.

Q: Can I use patio screens to keep out other pests like wasps?
A: Yes. 18×16 mesh blocks wasps and bees. For very small insects like thrips, upgrade to 20×20 mesh.

Q: How long does installation take for a large patio?
A> A professional crew can screen a typical 20×10 foot patio in 1‑2 days. DIY may take a weekend.
