Roll Up Mosquito Screen – Compact, Retractable Protection for Windows & Doors

When you want insect protection that is completely invisible when not in use, a roll up mosquito screen (also called a retractable screen) is the ideal solution. The mesh rolls into a slim cassette mounted at the top or side of your window or door, disappearing entirely when retracted. Pull it down or across only when needed. MBORMC manufactures custom roll‑up screens with smooth spring‑assisted operation, durable PET or stainless steel mesh, and a choice of cassette colours. In this guide, we’ll explain how roll‑up screens work, their advantages, and how to install one yourself.

What Is a Roll Up Mosquito Screen?

A roll up mosquito screen consists of a mesh that is wound around a spring‑loaded roller inside a compact cassette. The cassette can be mounted horizontally (above a door or window) so the screen pulls down, or vertically (at the side) so the screen pulls across. When you pull the screen, the spring tension unrolls the mesh. A latch at the opposite side holds it closed. To retract, you release the latch and the spring automatically rolls the mesh back into the cassette. The cassette is typically 2–3 inches wide and can be colour‑matched to your frame, making it nearly invisible.

Advantages of Roll Up Mosquito Screens

  • Completely hidden when not in use: No visible screen or stack – just a slim cassette.
  • Preserves your view: When retracted, you have an unobstructed window or doorway.
  • Ideal for doors: Retractable screens are perfect for entry doors where you don't want a permanent screen.
  • Easy to use: One‑handed operation – just pull and release.
  • No bottom track required for some models: Some roll‑up screens use a tension wire or magnetic strip instead of a floor track.

Types of Roll Up Screens from MBORMC

  • Top‑mount (vertical roll‑up): Cassette above the opening, screen pulls down. Best for windows and standard doors.
  • Side‑mount (horizontal roll‑up): Cassette at the side, screen pulls across. Ideal for sliding doors or wide windows.
  • Bi‑parting (two cassettes): Two screens that meet in the middle. For extra‑wide openings up to 4 metres.
  • Hands‑free magnetic latch: For sliding doors, the screen can be equipped with a magnetic catch that engages automatically.

Mesh Material Options

We offer roll‑up screens with PET fiber mesh (lightweight, smooth rolling) – standard for most residential applications. For high‑traffic areas or homes with pets, we recommend 304 stainless steel mesh. Note: stainless steel mesh is stiffer; it requires a stronger spring and may not roll as compactly, but it is extremely durable. Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black. Darker mesh reduces glare and improves outward visibility.

DIY Installation – Step by Step

Installing a roll‑up mosquito screen is a straightforward DIY project:

  • Measure the width and height of your window or door opening. For side‑mount, measure the width of the opening.
  • Order the custom screen from MBORMC with your exact dimensions, specifying top‑mount or side‑mount.
  • Mount the cassette using screws (wall anchors provided). Use a level to ensure it’s straight.
  • For top‑mount, attach the pull bar and install the side guides (if included).
  • For side‑mount, attach the top and bottom tracks.
  • Test the operation – the screen should roll smoothly and latch securely.

Basic tools required: drill, screwdriver, level, measuring tape. Total time: 30–45 minutes.

Custom Sizing – Perfect for Any Opening

MBORMC manufactures each roll‑up screen to your exact measurements. You provide width and height (in cm or inches). We cut the cassette and mesh precisely. Maximum width for a single cassette is 1.5m (for smooth spring operation). For wider openings, use a bi‑parting system or a side‑mount horizontal roll‑up (which can go up to 2m).

Where to Use Roll Up Screens

  • Kitchen windows: Retract the screen when cooking, deploy when you want fresh air.
  • Front entry doors: Keep mosquitoes out when bringing in groceries.
  • Patio doors: A side‑mount roll‑up screen slides across the opening.
  • Balcony doors in apartments: Space‑saving and aesthetically pleasing.
  • French doors: Use two separate cassettes (one per door) or a bi‑parting system.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does the spring mechanism last?
A: Our springs are rated for over 10,000 cycles, equivalent to daily use for 27 years.

Q: Can I install a roll‑up screen on a window that opens outward?
A: For outward‑opening windows, use a side‑mount cassette (pulls across) so that the screen does not block the opening sash.

Q: How do I clean the screen?
A: Vacuum with a brush attachment. For deep cleaning, unclip the screen from the sash (most models allow removal) and rinse with mild soap and water.

Q: Does the screen block airflow?
A: Our 18×16 PET mesh has ~85% openness, allowing excellent airflow. The optional 20×20 mesh has ~75% openness, still very good.
