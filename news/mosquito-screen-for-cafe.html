Mosquito Screen for Cafe – Create a Bug‑Free Outdoor Dining Experience

Published on May 6, 2026 | By MBORMC Team
Cafe Mosquito Screen Restaurant Screen Commercial Screen Retractable Screen Folding Screen

Cafes thrive on outdoor seating – the aroma of coffee, fresh air, and a relaxed atmosphere. But flies, wasps, and mosquitoes can ruin the experience for customers and lead to health code violations. A mosquito screen for cafe allows you to keep doors and windows open for ventilation while creating a bug‑free environment. MBORMC offers custom screens for cafe patios, service windows, entrance doors, and even full‑height glass openings. In this guide, we’ll cover the best screen solutions for cafes, how to choose durable materials for food service areas, and installation tips.

Why Your Cafe Needs Mosquito Screens

Cafes are especially vulnerable to insects because of food odors, open trash bins, and frequent door openings. Flies can transmit bacteria; wasps can sting; mosquitoes can bite. Health inspectors pay close attention to insect control. A single fly landing on a pastry can turn off a customer. Screens provide a physical barrier that:

  • Complies with health department regulations.
  • Enhances customer comfort, encouraging longer stays.
  • Reduces the need for chemical sprays or zappers near food.
  • Allows natural ventilation, saving on air conditioning costs.

Best Screen Types for Cafe Openings

  • Retractable Roll‑Up Screens – Ideal for front entrance doors or patio openings where you want a clean, unobstructed look when the screen is not in use. They roll into a slim cassette and can be operated by staff easily.
  • Fixed Frame Screens – For service windows, kitchen pass‑throughs, or windows that are always open. Durable and cost‑effective.
  • Folding (Pleated) Screens – For wide patio openings or entire walls that open up. Can be configured to stack on one or both sides.
  • Sliding Screen Doors – For sliding glass doors leading to a patio. Heavy‑duty sliding screens with stainless steel mesh withstand constant use.
  • Magnetic Strip Curtains – For walk‑in coolers or back kitchen doors, these clear PVC strips keep insects out while allowing staff to pass through with trays.

Mesh Material – Why 304 Stainless Steel is Essential for Cafes

For cafes, 304 stainless steel mesh is the only practical choice. It is:

  • Fire‑resistant – important for kitchen safety.
  • Easy to clean – can be scrubbed with food‑safe degreasers.
  • Pet‑proof and impact‑resistant – withstands delivery carts and busy staff.
  • Corrosion‑resistant – ideal for outdoor patios and humid environments.

PET mesh is not recommended for commercial food service as it can absorb grease, tear easily, and is not fire‑resistant. Stainless steel mesh is available in 18×16 or 20×20 density, in dark grey or black. Dark mesh reduces glare and is less noticeable.

Custom Sizing for Unique Cafe Layouts

We manufacture screens to fit any opening – from small coffee bar service windows to large storefront openings. Provide width and height in cm or inches. For arched or shaped openings, we can create custom frames. Maximum width for folding screens: 4m; for retractable: 3m; for sliding: 3m. Larger openings can be divided into multiple panels.

Installation Options for Cafes

For busy cafes, we recommend professional installation to minimize downtime. Our installers can work during off‑hours. For smaller cafes, DIY installation is possible – all screens come with detailed instructions. We also offer on‑site training for your maintenance staff.

Maintaining a Clean Appearance

Screens should be cleaned weekly to remove dust and grease. Stainless steel mesh can be scrubbed with a soft brush and mild detergent. Frames can be wiped with a damp cloth. We offer spare mesh and spline for quick repairs if damaged.
