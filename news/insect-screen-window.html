Insect Screen Window – Comprehensive Protection Against All Flying Insects

Published on May 4, 2026 | By MBORMC Team

Flying insects come in many shapes and sizes – from disease‑carrying mosquitoes to nuisance flies and stinging wasps. A dedicated insect screen window is designed to block them all, providing comprehensive protection while preserving fresh air and natural light. MBORMC manufactures custom insect screens for every window type: casement, sliding, double‑hung, awning, tilt‑and‑turn, and even arched or circular windows. In this guide, we’ll explain how all‑in‑one insect screens differ from species‑specific screens, which mesh density to choose, and how to install them yourself.

Why “Insect Screen” Instead of Mosquito or Fly Screen?

While a mosquito screen may block mosquitoes but let tiny midges through, and a fly screen may block flies but not mosquitoes, a true insect screen uses a fine mesh (18×16 or 20×20 threads per inch) that stops nearly all flying insects – from mosquitoes and flies to wasps, bees, midges, sand flies, and even small moths. It’s the most versatile choice for households with multiple insect threats. If you live near lakes, rivers, farms, or wooded areas, an all‑purpose insect screen window is your best defense.

Types of Insect Screen Windows from MBORMC

  • Fixed frame screens: Rigid screen mounted directly to the window frame. Most durable and provides a tight seal. Ideal for windows you open frequently.
  • Retractable roll‑up screens: The mesh rolls into a slim cassette at the top or side. Disappears when not in use. Great for windows you only open occasionally.
  • Sliding screens: For horizontal sliding windows, a sliding screen moves on a track. Available as rigid frame or pleated.
  • Pleated (folding) screens: Accordion‑fold mesh that stacks at one side. Perfect for wide windows where cassettes are impractical.
  • Magnetic screens: Attaches with magnetic strips – no drilling, tool‑free. Ideal for renters or temporary use.

Mesh Density: 18×16 vs. 20×20

  • 18×16 mesh (standard): Blocks mosquitoes, house flies, wasps, large midges. Suitable for most regions where no‑see‑ums are not a problem.
  • 20×20 mesh (anti‑mosquito grade): Blocks Aedes aegypti (dengue mosquito), Anopheles, Culex, and most biting midges (no‑see‑ums). Recommended for tropical areas and disease‑endemic regions.

For areas with sand flies or tiny biting midges, 20×20 is essential. For general use, 18×16 offers a good balance of protection and airflow.

Mesh Material: PET vs. 304 Stainless Steel

PET fiber mesh: Lightweight, excellent transparency (85%+), UV‑resistant, rust‑proof, lasts 5–10 years. Ideal for most residential windows.

304 stainless steel mesh: Extremely strong, pet‑proof (resists scratching), fire‑resistant, lasts 10–20 years. Slightly less transparent (75%+). Recommended for ground‑floor windows or homes with pets.

Both materials available in grey, dark grey, or black. Darker mesh reduces glare and improves outward visibility.

Window Type Compatibility

  • Casement / awning windows: Fixed interior frame screen is best. Retractable top‑mount also works.
  • Sliding windows: Sliding screen (rigid or pleated) or side‑mount retractable.
  • Double‑hung windows: Fixed frame screen over the entire opening, or separate screens for upper and lower sashes.
  • Tilt‑and‑turn windows: Fixed interior frame screen – ensure clearance for tilting.
  • Arched / round windows: Custom‑shaped fixed frame screen – we manufacture to your template.

How to Measure for an Insect Screen Window

  • Measure the width and height of the window opening at three points each. Use the smallest measurements.
  • For fixed frames, subtract 2‑3mm from each dimension for easy fit. For Z‑frames (spring clips), order the exact opening size.
  • For arched windows: provide the width at the widest point, total height, and a tracing or photo with a ruler.

DIY Installation – Simple Steps

All MBORMC insect screen windows are designed for easy DIY installation:

  • Fixed frame: Clean the window frame, then place the screen into the opening and secure with screws or spring clips.
  • Retractable: Mount the cassette, attach side guides, then pull the screen down and latch.
  • Sliding: Install top and bottom tracks, then slide the panel in.
  • Magnetic: Apply adhesive magnetic tape to the window frame, then press the screen onto it.

Basic tools (drill, screwdriver) may be needed for some types. Detailed instructions included.
