Anti Mosquito Screen – Specialized Defense Against Disease Vectors

Published on May 1, 2026 | By MBORMC Team
Anti Mosquito Screen Mosquito Screen Insect Screen Mosquito Net Screen Retractable Screen

Mosquitoes are not just a nuisance – they are the deadliest animals on Earth, transmitting diseases like dengue, malaria, Zika, chikungunya, and West Nile virus. An anti mosquito screen is a specialized physical barrier designed to block all species of mosquitoes – from the tiny Aedes aegypti (dengue vector) to the larger Anopheles (malaria vector). MBORMC manufactures custom anti mosquito screens for windows, doors, and other openings, using high‑density mesh that mosquitoes cannot penetrate. Unlike chemical repellents or insecticides, a screen provides 24/7, non‑toxic protection. In this guide, we’ll discuss why mosquito‑specific screens are essential, the optimal mesh density, and how to install them yourself.

Why Choose an Anti Mosquito Screen Over General Insect Screens?

General insect screens use mesh with apertures of around 1.2mm (18×16 threads per inch). This blocks house flies and most mosquitoes, but some tiny mosquito species (especially Aedes aegypti, the dengue mosquito) can squeeze through if the mesh is not fine enough. An anti mosquito screen uses a tighter weave – typically 20×20 or even 24×24 threads per inch – with apertures as small as 0.6mm. This ensures that even the smallest mosquito cannot pass. Additionally, anti mosquito screens are often made with materials that discourage mosquito resting (e.g., dark colors, specific surface textures). MBORMC offers a specialized “Anti‑Mosquito” upgrade with 20×20 PET or stainless steel mesh and a black finish that reduces attraction.

Mosquito Species and Mesh Requirements

  • Aedes aegypti (dengue, Zika, chikungunya): Very small – requires 20×20 mesh or finer.
  • Anopheles (malaria): Medium size – 18×16 mesh is sufficient but 20×20 adds extra safety.
  • Culex (West Nile, filariasis): Medium to large – 18×16 mesh works.
  • Mansonia (arboviruses): Larger – 16×14 mesh may suffice, but for universal protection, use 20×20.

MBORMC’s standard PET mesh is 18×16, which blocks most mosquitoes. Our “Anti‑Mosquito” upgrade uses 20×20 stainless steel or PET, offering near‑absolute protection. For areas with high dengue risk, we strongly recommend the upgrade.

Material: PET vs. 304 Stainless Steel for Anti Mosquito Applications

Both materials can be woven to 20×20 density. PET fiber mesh is lightweight, affordable, and offers excellent transparency (85% openness). PET is UV‑stabilized and rust‑proof, with a lifespan of 5–10 years. It’s available in dark grey or black – recommended because mosquitoes are less attracted to dark colors. 304 stainless steel mesh is extremely strong, pet‑proof, and lasts 10–20 years. It’s slightly less transparent (about 75%) but provides superior tear resistance. For anti mosquito screens in high‑risk areas, either material works; choose based on your budget and durability needs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Anti Mosquito Screens

Q: Do I really need a special anti mosquito screen?
A: If you live in a dengue‑endemic area or have seen tiny mosquitoes (Aedes) in your home, yes. Standard insect screens may not block them. Our 20×20 mesh stops even the smallest species.

Q: Will a finer mesh reduce airflow?
A: Slightly, but the difference is minor. 20×20 mesh has about 75% openness vs 85% for 18×16. You’ll still get plenty of breeze.

Q: Can I retrofit my existing screen door with anti mosquito mesh?
A: Possibly. Contact us with your frame dimensions; we can supply just the mesh and spline for you to re‑screen your existing frame.

Q: Are anti mosquito screens effective against sand flies (no‑see‑ums)?
A: Yes. 20×20 mesh also blocks most biting midges. For severe no‑see‑um infestations, we offer 24×24 mesh on request.
