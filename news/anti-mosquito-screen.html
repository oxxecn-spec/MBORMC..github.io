Anti Mosquito Screen – Your First Line of Defense Against Mosquito-Borne Diseases

Published on May 1, 2026 | By MBORMC Team
Anti Mosquito Screen Mosquito Screen Insect Screen Retractable Screen Velcro Screen

Mosquitoes are the deadliest animals on earth, responsible for over 700,000 deaths annually from malaria, dengue, Zika, chikungunya, and other diseases. An anti mosquito screen is a simple, chemical‑free, and highly effective way to protect your family. MBORMC offers custom‑made anti mosquito screens that fit any window or door, blocking all common mosquito species. In this guide, we explain the science behind mosquito protection, screen material options, and how our DIY screens can be installed in minutes.

Why You Need a Dedicated Anti Mosquito Screen

Standard fly screens may have mesh openings too large for small mosquito species like Aedes aegypti (the dengue mosquito) or Anopheles (malaria mosquito). An effective anti mosquito screen must have an aperture of 1.2mm or smaller – typically 18×16 threads per inch or finer. MBORMC screens are designed specifically to meet this standard. They keep mosquitoes out while allowing maximum airflow and visibility.

Protecting against mosquitoes is especially critical in:

  • Tropical and subtropical regions (Africa, Southeast Asia, Latin America) where malaria and dengue are endemic.
  • Subtropical and temperate areas where West Nile virus, Japanese encephalitis, or locally transmitted dengue occur.
  • Homes with infants, elderly, or immunocompromised individuals who are more vulnerable to mosquito-borne diseases.
  • Travelers returning from high-risk areas who may bring infected mosquitoes into their homes.

Types of Anti Mosquito Screens from MBORMC

  • Retractable Roll‑Up Screens: Ideal for windows and doors where you want the screen to disappear when not in use. The mesh rolls into a slim cassette.
  • Folding Concertina Screens: Perfect for wide openings like French doors and large windows. Folds neatly to the side.
  • Fixed Frame Screens: Permanent solution with an aluminum frame attached to the window or door frame. Most durable.
  • Self‑Adhesive Velcro Screens: No‑drill, tool‑free option for renters or temporary use. Attach with tape – remove in winter.
  • Magnetic Mesh Curtains: Walk‑through screen for doors; snaps shut with magnets. Ideal for patios and back doors.

Material Options: PET vs. 304 Stainless Steel

Both materials are fine enough to block all mosquito species:

  • PET Fiber Mesh: Lightweight, affordable, and breathable. Offers >85% openness for clear visibility. UV‑stabilized, rust‑proof. Available in grey, dark grey, or black. Lifespan 5–10 years. Ideal for most homes.
  • 304 Stainless Steel Mesh: Heavy‑duty, pet‑proof, and extremely durable (10–20 years). Slightly lower transparency (~75%) but offers superior strength. Best for ground‑floor windows, doors in high‑traffic areas, or where pets may scratch.

Both meshes have an aperture of 1.2mm or less, tested to block Aedes aegypti, Anopheles, and Culex species. We recommend pairing with a close‑fitting frame to eliminate any gaps where mosquitoes could enter.

Frame Colours & Customisation

Our screens feature high‑quality 6063‑T5 aluminum frames, powder‑coated in any RAL colour. Popular options include white, grey, black, bronze, and wood grain. You can match your existing window frames so the screen blends in virtually invisibly. For Velcro and magnetic screens, the frame is flexible PVC or simply the mesh itself – perfect for renters.

DIY Installation – No Professional Needed

All MBORMC anti mosquito screens are designed for easy DIY installation. Each order includes a step‑by‑step guide with illustrations. Installation steps vary by type:

  • Fixed frame screens: Screw or clip into the window/door frame. Requires a screwdriver. Takes about 15 minutes per window.
  • Retractable screens: Mount the cassette using screws and wall anchors. Install the guide rails. Takes about 30 minutes per door/window.
  • Folding screens: Mount the top rail and bottom track. Folds from a side stack. Takes 30–45 minutes.
  • Velcro or magnetic screens: No tools – clean the surface, apply tape or magnets, and press the screen on. Takes 5 minutes.

We provide all necessary hardware. If you can measure and use a basic drill or screwdriver, you can install any of our screens.

Frequently Asked Questions About Anti Mosquito Screens

Q: Can a mosquito screen block the very small Aedes aegypti mosquito?
A: Yes. Our standard 18×16 mesh has an aperture of approximately 1.2mm, which is smaller than the average Aedes aegypti (2–4mm). For extra assurance, we can supply 20×20 mesh.

Q: Will the screen reduce airflow?
A: Our PET mesh has over 85% openness, which minimally affects airflow. You’ll still feel a strong breeze.

Q: How do I clean an anti mosquito screen?
A: Vacuum with a brush attachment, or wipe with a damp cloth. For deep cleaning, remove Velcro/magnetic screens and rinse with mild soap and water.

Q: Can I use an anti mosquito screen on a sliding door?
A: Yes. Our retractable roll‑up and folding concertina screens are designed specifically for sliding doors. They operate smoothly and do not interfere with the door’s movement.

Q: Do you offer screens for humanitarian or public health programs?
