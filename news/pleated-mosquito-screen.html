Pleated Mosquito Screen – Accordion-Style Foldable Protection for Wide Openings

Wide openings like French doors, large windows, and patio entrances require specialised insect screens. A pleated mosquito screen (also called a concertina or folding screen) uses an accordion‑fold mesh that stacks compactly at one or both sides when open, and expands to cover the entire opening when closed. MBORMC manufactures custom pleated screens for openings up to 4 meters wide, with smooth gliding and durable construction. In this guide, we’ll explain how pleated screens work, their advantages over retractable roll‑ups, and how you can install one yourself.

What Is a Pleated Mosquito Screen?

A pleated mosquito screen consists of a mesh that is folded like a paper fan. The folds allow the screen to be very compact when closed (stacked at the side) and expand horizontally or vertically to cover the opening. The screen rides on a top rail and bottom track (or just a tension wire) and is operated by a handle. When not in use, the screen collapses into a stack that is typically 5–10 cm wide, taking up very little space. Pleated screens are ideal for openings that are too wide for a single retractable cassette (which maxes out around 1.5m).

Advantages of Pleated vs. Retractable Screens

  • Extra wide openings: Pleated screens can handle widths up to 4 meters (13 feet) in a single unit.
  • No cassette: Without a bulky cassette, pleated screens can be top‑mounted or side‑mounted with minimal visible hardware.
  • Bi‑parting options: Two pleated screens can meet in the middle for very large double doors.
  • Smooth operation: The screen glides along a track, requiring less force than a retractable spring.
  • Customizable stack direction: You can have the screen stack on the left, right, or both sides.

The trade‑off: pleated screens do not retract fully into a hidden cassette – the folded stack remains visible, though it is quite thin. For most applications, this is acceptable.

Types of Pleated Screens from MBORMC

  • Horizontal sliding pleated screen: The most common type, used for doors and wide windows. The screen slides left‑right.
  • Vertical sliding pleated screen: For tall, narrow openings; the screen stacks at the top or bottom. Rare.
  • Bi‑parting (center‑meeting): Two pleated screens that stack on both sides and meet in the middle. Ideal for double French doors.
  • Top‑mount only (no bottom track): For applications where a floor track is undesirable (e.g., threshold). The screen uses a tension wire or weighted bottom.

Mesh Material Options

We offer pleated screens with PET fiber mesh (lightweight, UV‑resistant, 18×16 density) – standard for residential use. For high‑traffic areas or where pets may scratch, we recommend 304 stainless steel mesh. Stainless steel is also available in a pleated configuration, though it is slightly stiffer than PET. Both materials come in grey, dark grey, or black. For pet owners, stainless steel is highly recommended as it resists tearing from claws.

DIY Installation – Step by Step

Installing a pleated screen is a DIY‑friendly project. Here’s the general process:

  • Measure the width and height of your opening. For French doors, measure each leaf individually.
  • Order the custom pleated screen from MBORMC with your exact dimensions and stacking direction (left, right, or both).
  • Install the top rail (and bottom track if applicable) using screws. Use a level to ensure it’s straight.
  • Attach the screen assembly to the rail – it clips into place.
  • Install the pull handle and latch.
  • Test sliding – it should move smoothly without binding.

Basic tools required: drill, screwdriver, level, measuring tape. Total time: 30–60 minutes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How durable is the pleated mesh?
A: With proper use, PET pleated screens last 5–8 years; stainless steel lasts 10–20 years. The folds may wear over time, but replacement mesh can be ordered.

Q: Can I install a pleated screen on an outward‑opening door?
A: Yes. For outward‑opening doors, the screen must be mounted on the interior side. Ensure the door swing does not hit the screen when open.

Q: How do I clean a pleated screen?
A: Use a soft brush or vacuum with a brush attachment. Do not machine wash. For deep cleaning, gently wipe with a damp cloth.

Q: What is the difference between a pleated screen and a retractable screen?
A: A retractable screen rolls into a cassette and disappears completely. A pleated screen folds into a stack that remains visible. Pleated screens can be much wider.
