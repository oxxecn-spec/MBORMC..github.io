Folding Mosquito Screen – Compact, Accordion-Style Protection for Wide Openings

Published on May 3, 2026 | By MBORMC Team

If you have a wide patio door, French doors, or a large window opening, a standard retractable or fixed screen may not be practical. A folding mosquito screen (also called a concertina or pleated screen) is the ideal solution for wide openings. The mesh folds like an accordion, stacking compactly at one or both sides when open, and expanding to cover the entire opening when closed. MBORMC manufactures custom folding screens for openings up to 4 meters wide. In this guide, we’ll explain how folding screens work, their advantages over other types, and how to install one yourself.

What Is a Folding Mosquito Screen?

A folding mosquito screen consists of a pleated mesh that is hung on a top rail (and sometimes a bottom track). The mesh is pre‑pleated so that it can be compressed into a narrow stack (typically 5–10 cm wide) at one side or both sides. A handle allows you to pull the screen across the opening, and magnets or clips keep it closed. When not in use, the screen collapses back into its stack, leaving the opening clear. Because there is no bulky cassette, folding screens can be made much wider than retractable roll‑up screens.

Advantages of Folding Mosquito Screens

  • Extra‑wide coverage: Single folding screens up to 4 meters (13 feet) wide.
  • Bi‑parting option: Two screens that meet in the middle for symmetrical operation.
  • No cassette: The folded stack is slim and less obtrusive than a retractable cassette.
  • Smooth gliding: Rides on a top rail with ball‑bearing sliders.
  • Easy to operate: Pull with one hand – no spring tension to fight.

Types of Folding Screens from MBORMC

  • Single‑stack (one‑way): The screen folds to the left or right. Best for single doors or windows.
  • Bi‑parting (center‑meeting): Two screens that stack on both sides and meet in the middle. Ideal for French doors and very wide openings.
  • Top‑mount only (no bottom track): For thresholds where a floor track is undesirable – the screen uses a weighted bottom bar.
  • With magnetic closure: Magnets along the leading edge ensure a tight seal.

Mesh Material Options

We offer folding screens with PET fiber mesh (standard, lightweight, 18×16 density) – perfect for most residential use. For high‑traffic areas or homes with pets, 304 stainless steel mesh is available (heavier but extremely durable). Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black. PET is easier to fold and lighter; stainless steel offers better scratch resistance.

DIY Installation – Step by Step

Installing a folding mosquito screen is a straightforward DIY project. Here’s the process:

  • Measure the width and height of your door or window opening.
  • Order the custom folding screen from MBORMC with your exact dimensions, stacking direction, and track preference.
  • Install the top rail using screws (wall anchors provided). Use a level to ensure it’s perfectly horizontal.
  • If using a bottom track, install it aligned with the top rail.
  • Attach the folding screen assembly to the top rail – it clips into the sliders.
  • Install the handle and latch or magnetic catch at the leading edge.
  • Test sliding – it should move smoothly without binding. Adjust the tension if needed.

Basic tools required: drill, screwdriver, level, measuring tape. Total time: 30–60 minutes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How durable is the pleated mesh?
A> PET pleated screens typically last 5–8 years with normal use. Stainless steel lasts 10–20 years. Replacement mesh can be ordered.

Q: Can I install a folding screen on an outward‑opening door?
A: Yes, as long as the screen is mounted on the interior side. Ensure the door swing does not hit the screen.

Q: How do I clean a folding screen?
A: Vacuum with a brush attachment. For deeper cleaning, wipe with a damp cloth. Do not machine wash or submerge.

Q: What’s the difference between a folding screen and a pleated screen?
A: They are the same – “folding” and “pleated” are used interchangeably. Both refer to accordion‑style screens.
