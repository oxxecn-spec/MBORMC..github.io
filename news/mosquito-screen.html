What Is a Mosquito Screen?

A mosquito screen (also called a mosquito net or insect screen) is a mesh barrier fitted to windows, doors, or other openings to prevent mosquitoes, flies, and other flying insects from entering a building while allowing fresh air and natural light to pass through. Screens can be fixed, retractable, folding, or magnetic. MBORMC specializes in custom‑made screens that are cut precisely to your window or door dimensions – no standard sizes, no gaps.

Why You Need a Mosquito Screen in Every Room

Mosquitoes are not just a nuisance; they spread diseases like dengue, malaria, Zika, and chikungunya. In tropical and subtropical regions, the risk is especially high. Even in temperate climates, summer evenings can be ruined by buzzing mosquitoes. A mosquito screen provides a chemical‑free, 24/7 barrier. It also keeps out flies, wasps, midges, and moths. For homes with children, elderly, or pets, physical protection is the safest method.

Materials – PET Mesh vs. 304 Stainless Steel

MBORMC uses two primary mesh materials. PET fiber mesh is lightweight, breathable, and affordable. It provides excellent insect protection while maintaining high transparency and airflow. PET is UV‑stabilized and won’t rust. 304 stainless steel mesh is incredibly strong, pet‑proof, and fire‑resistant. It’s ideal for ground‑floor windows or areas where pets may scratch. Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black, and can be matched to any frame colour (white, black, bronze, wood grain, etc.).

Frequently Asked Questions About Mosquito Screens

Q: Can I leave my mosquito screen installed year‑round?
A: Yes. PET mesh and stainless steel are UV‑resistant and weather‑proof. They will not degrade under sun or rain.

Q: How do I clean the screen?
A: Use a soft brush or vacuum with a brush attachment. For deeper cleaning, remove the screen (Velcro or clip systems) and rinse with mild soap and water.

Q: Are your screens pet‑proof?
A: Our 304 stainless steel mesh is highly resistant to scratching and biting. PET mesh is less durable against pets, so we recommend stainless steel for homes with dogs or cats.
