If you have dogs or cats, you know how quickly they can damage standard insect screens. A single paw swipe or excited jump can tear holes in PET mesh, rendering your screen useless. A pet proof mosquito screen uses heavy‑duty 304 stainless steel mesh that resists scratching, biting, and tearing. In this guide, we’ll explain why stainless steel is the only choice for pet owners, how to choose the right screen style, and installation tips.

Why Standard Mesh Fails with Pets

Conventional PET or fiberglass mesh has a tensile strength of around 50–70 lbs per inch. A dog’s claw can easily puncture it, and a determined cat can shred it in minutes. Once torn, the screen no longer blocks insects, and the hole can enlarge with further scratching. Repairing PET mesh is difficult; often the entire screen must be replaced. Pet owners need a material that is physically stronger than their pet’s claws and teeth – that material is 304 stainless steel.

304 Stainless Steel Mesh – The Gold Standard for Pet Proofing

304 stainless steel mesh is woven from high‑tensile wire. It has the following advantages:

  • Tensile strength: Over 200 lbs per inch – claws will not penetrate.
  • Bite‑resistant: Dogs cannot chew through stainless steel wire.
  • Corrosion‑resistant: 304 grade is suitable for indoor and outdoor use, even in coastal areas.
  • Fire‑resistant: Will not burn or melt.
  • Long lifespan: 10–20 years, even with daily pet interaction.

The trade‑off: stainless steel mesh is slightly less transparent (about 75% openness vs 85% for PET) and costs more. But for pet owners, the durability is well worth the investment.

Screen Styles Available in Pet‑Proof Stainless Steel

MBORMC can manufacture any of our screen styles with 304 stainless steel mesh:

  • Fixed frame screens: Most durable option – the rigid aluminum frame adds structural strength.
  • Retractable roll‑up screens: Available with stainless steel mesh, though the heavier mesh may require a stronger spring. Contact us for availability.
  • Sliding screens (rigid frame): Excellent for sliding patio doors – the sliding action is unaffected by the heavier mesh.
  • Folding (pleated) screens: Stainless steel pleated mesh is stiffer than PET but works well for wide openings.
  • Magnetic screens: Not recommended with stainless steel mesh because the weight may cause sagging.

Choosing the Right Stainless Steel Mesh Density

We offer 304 stainless steel in two densities:

  • 18×16 mesh: Standard density, blocks mosquitoes, flies, wasps. Adequate for most pet owners.
  • 20×20 mesh: Finer mesh, blocks no‑see‑ums and tiny midges. Slightly heavier but offers maximum insect protection.

Both options are available in dark grey or black. The darker colour reduces glare and is less visible from the outside.

Frame Considerations for Pet‑Proof Screens

Stainless steel mesh is heavier than PET, so the frame must be robust. We recommend:

  • Heavy‑duty frame profile (30mm × 15mm) for large screens or doors.
  • Powder‑coated finish to resist scratching from paws.
  • Screw‑mount installation rather than clips for maximum stability.

For sliding screens, ensure the track can handle the weight – our standard tracks are rated for stainless steel screens.

DIY Installation Tips for Pet‑Proof Screens

Installing a stainless steel screen is similar to a standard screen, but the mesh is stiffer. Use these tips:

  • Cut the mesh with heavy‑duty tin snips (not scissors).
  • Wear gloves – wire ends can be sharp.
  • Use a spline roller with extra force to press the mesh into the frame.
  • For retractable screens, we pre‑assemble the cassette – no DIY mesh cutting required.

If you’re not comfortable working with stainless steel mesh, order pre‑assembled screens from MBORMC – we do all the fabrication for you.

Where to Install Pet‑Proof Screens

  • Doors that pets use frequently: Patio doors, back doors, pet doors.
  • Ground‑floor windows: Where pets can reach and scratch.
  • Sunrooms or porches: Where pets spend time.
  • All windows if you have large, energetic dogs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will stainless steel mesh rust?
A: 304 grade is highly corrosion‑resistant, but if scratched deeply, it may show minor rust spots. For coastal homes, consider 316 marine grade (available on request).

Q: Can a dog still damage a stainless steel screen?
A: It is very unlikely. A determined large dog might bend the frame but not tear the mesh. Use a heavy‑duty frame.

Q: Is stainless steel mesh safe for cats?
A: Yes. Cats cannot scratch through it, and it poses no hazard.

Q: Can I replace my existing PET mesh with stainless steel?
A: Yes, if the frame is strong enough. Contact us for a replacement mesh kit.
