Door Mosquito Screen – Protect Every Entrance from Insects

Published on May 1, 2026 | By MBORMC Team
Door Mosquito Screen Mosquito Screen Insect Screen Folding Screen Door Magnetic Curtain

Doors are the largest openings in your home – and the most vulnerable to insect intrusion. A high‑quality door mosquito screen lets you keep doors open for ventilation, easy access, and natural light while keeping mosquitoes, flies, and other pests out. MBORMC offers custom‑made door screens for all door types: sliding patio doors, French doors, main entry doors, balcony doors, and even pet doors. In this guide, we’ll help you choose the best solution for your door and explain the simple DIY installation process.

Why Install a Door Mosquito Screen?

Unlike windows, doors are opened and closed frequently. A door screen must be durable, easy to use, and able to withstand repeated operation. Without a screen, every time you open a door in the evening, you invite mosquitoes and moths inside. Door screens provide a barrier that allows free movement while blocking insects. They are especially important for:

  • Patio and garden doors – often left open during summer evenings.
  • French doors – wide openings that need a screen that can accommodate both leaves.
  • Main entrance doors – to prevent flies from entering when bringing in groceries or welcoming guests.
  • Balcony doors – in apartments, where fresh air is precious but mosquitoes are abundant.
  • Pet doors – to keep bugs out while allowing pets to come and go.

Types of Door Mosquito Screens from MBORMC

We offer several screen styles designed specifically for doors:

  • Retractable Roll‑Up Door Screens: The screen slides horizontally (for sliding doors) or rolls vertically (for hinged doors) into a compact cassette. When not in use, the screen is completely hidden. Ideal for patio doors that are used frequently.
  • Folding Concertina Door Screens: The screen folds like an accordion to one or both sides. Perfect for double doors (French doors) where you need to open both leaves independently. Also great for wide openings up to 4 meters.
  • Magnetic Mesh Curtains: A simple walk‑through screen with a center magnetic split. You push it open and it snaps shut behind you. Best for low‑traffic doors or rental properties. No installation – just hang with hooks or adhesive tape.
  • Self‑Adhesive Velcro Door Screens: Similar to magnetic but uses Velcro closure. Also tool‑free and removable. Good for temporary use or seasonal protection.
  • Fixed Frame Door Screens: A permanent screen with an aluminum frame that attaches to the door frame. Best for doors that are not opened often, like side entry doors.

Door Type Compatibility

  • Sliding Patio Doors: Use a vertical retractable screen that mounts at the side, or a folding screen that stacks at one end. The screen must slide with the door or remain stationary. Our retractable screens are designed to slide on a rail that mounts to the floor or the door frame.
  • French Doors (double hinged): A folding concertina screen is the best choice. It can be installed as a single screen that pulls across both doors, or as two separate screens (one per door) that meet in the middle with a magnetic or finger‑pull seal.
  • Single Hinged Entry Doors: Use a retractable screen that rolls up into a vertical cassette mounted at the side. When you open the screen, it rolls up; when you close it, you pull the handle across the door opening and latch it at the other side.
  • Balcony Doors (sliding or swinging): Same as patio doors. For narrow balcony doors, a roll‑up screen works well.
  • Pet Doors: We can integrate a pet flap into a fixed screen, or you can install a separate pet‑sized Velcro screen next to the main door screen. Contact us for custom pet door solutions.

Material Options: PET vs. 304 Stainless Steel

MBORMC uses two primary mesh materials for door screens. PET fiber mesh is lightweight, breathable, and affordable. It provides excellent insect protection while maintaining high transparency (over 85% openness). PET is UV‑stabilized and won’t rust. It’s available in grey, dark grey, or black to match your door frame. 304 stainless steel mesh is incredibly strong, pet‑proof, and fire‑resistant. It’s ideal for ground‑floor doors or areas where pets may scratch. Stainless steel offers a more premium look and longer lifespan (10–20 years). For doors in high‑traffic areas, we recommend stainless steel for extra durability.

DIY Installation – No Professional Needed

All MBORMC door mosquito screens are designed for hassle‑free DIY installation. Each order includes a step‑by‑step guide with illustrations. Installation steps vary by type:

  • Retractable screens: You’ll mount the cassette at the side of the door using screws (wall anchors provided). Then run the guide rails along the top and bottom. The installation takes about 30–45 minutes.
  • Folding screens: You’ll mount a top rail and bottom track (or floor‑mounted guide). The screen folds from a side stack. Similar complexity to retractable screens.
  • Magnetic/Velcro curtains: You simply hang the curtain using hooks, nails, or adhesive tape. Takes less than 5 minutes. No tools required.

We provide all necessary hardware and detailed instructions. If you can use a drill and a screwdriver, you can install any of our door screens.

Frequently Asked Questions About Door Mosquito Screens

Q: Will a door screen interfere with my door’s operation?
A: No. Our screens are designed to not interfere. Retractable screens roll away to the side, folding screens stack out of the way, and magnetic curtains part in the middle. You can still open and close your door normally.

Q: Can I install a door screen on a door that opens outward?
A: Yes. For outward‑opening doors, we recommend a retractable screen that mounts inside the door frame, or a folding screen that sits flush. Provide your door’s opening direction when ordering.

Q: How do I clean a door screen?
A: For retractable and folding screens, use a soft brush or vacuum. For magnetic/velcro screens, they can be removed and rinsed with mild soap and water.

Q: Do you offer screens for dog or cat doors?
A: Yes. We can cut a flap opening in a fixed screen or provide a separate small Velcro screen for pet doors. Contact us for details.
