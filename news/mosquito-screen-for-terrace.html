Mosquito Screen for Terrace – Expand Your Living Space Outdoors

Published on May 5, 2026 | By MBORMC Team
Terrace Mosquito Screen Patio Door Screen Pleated Screen Retractable Screen Balcony Screen

A terrace is a wonderful extension of your home – a place to dine, relax, and entertain. But mosquitoes, flies, and other insects can quickly ruin the experience. A mosquito screen for terrace allows you to enclose all or part of your terrace, creating a bug‑free zone while preserving the open‑air feel. MBORMC offers custom screens for terrace openings, including large folding screens, retractable screens, and fixed panels. In this guide, we’ll explore different terrace screen solutions, how to choose the right system, and installation considerations.

Why Screen Your Terrace?

An unscreened terrace is often unusable during peak mosquito hours (dawn and dusk). Screens allow you to:

  • Enjoy meals, reading, or lounging without buzzing insects.
  • Keep children and pets safe from insect bites and stings.
  • Protect against mosquito‑borne diseases (dengue, malaria, Zika).
  • Reduce the need for chemical repellents or citronella candles.
  • Increase the usable square footage of your home.

Types of Terrace Screens

The best screen system depends on your terrace layout and how much openness you want.

  • Folding (Pleated) Screen Walls – Ideal for wide terrace openings (e.g., floor‑to‑ceiling glass walls). The screens stack at the sides like an accordion, allowing you to open the entire wall or just a section. Can be made up to 4m wide per unit.
  • Retractable Roll‑Up Screens – For single doorways or smaller openings. The screen rolls into a cassette at the top or side. When not needed, it disappears completely.
  • Sliding Screen Panels – Multiple rigid screens that slide on tracks, like sliding glass doors. Can be configured as 2‑panel, 3‑panel, or 4‑panel systems. Great for enclosing a porch or terrace.
  • Fixed Frame Screens – Permanent screens for sections of the terrace that you never need to open (e.g., above a railing). The most cost‑effective option but not retractable.

Mesh Material for Terrace Screens

For terrace screens that are exposed to weather and wind, choose 304 stainless steel mesh for maximum durability. Stainless steel resists tearing, rust, and UV damage, and lasts 10‑20 years. It also provides extra security, acting as a barrier against intrusion. For sheltered terraces, PET fiber mesh (18×16) is lighter and more affordable, with excellent visibility. Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black – dark mesh reduces glare and is less visible from outside.

Design Considerations

  • Track systems: Folding and sliding screens require top and bottom tracks. Ensure your terrace floor and ceiling can accommodate them.
  • Corner solutions: For L‑shaped terraces, we can design corner‑connecting tracks or use separate screen systems.
  • Wind resistance: Stainless steel mesh handles wind better than PET. In high‑wind areas, add additional clips or reinforced frames.
  • Colour matching: Our frames can be powder‑coated to match your terrace railing or door frames.

How to Measure for a Terrace Screen

  • Measure the width and height of the opening to be screened. For multiple sections, measure each individually.
  • For folding screens, we need the total width and height. We will calculate the number of panels and stack size.
  • For sliding panels, measure the overall width; we will specify the number of panels and track length.
  • If your terrace has an irregular shape (arched, angled), provide a template or detailed drawing.

DIY vs. Professional Installation

Small terrace screens (single door) can be DIY. But for large folding or sliding screen walls, professional installation is recommended due to the weight and alignment requirements. MBORMC can provide detailed installation instructions, or we can recommend local contractors. For new construction, we can work with your builder to integrate screens into the design.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I leave terrace screens up all year?
A: Yes, stainless steel screens are weather‑proof. PET screens can also be left up year‑round in mild climates.

Q: Do terrace screens block the view?
A: High‑transparency PET mesh offers over 85% openness; stainless steel offers about 75%. Your view will be slightly tinted but very clear.

Q: How do I clean terrace screens?
A: Hose them down or use a soft brush. For stubborn dirt, use mild soap and water.

Q: Can I get motorized screens for a large terrace?
A: Yes, motorized retractable screens are available for terrace openings up to 6m wide. Contact us for a quote.
