Fly Screen Door – Keep Flies, Wasps & Bees Out While You Enjoy Fresh Air

Published on May 3, 2026 | By MBORMC Team

Flies, wasps, and bees are more than just annoying – they can contaminate food, spread bacteria, and cause painful stings. A fly screen door provides a simple, chemical‑free barrier that keeps these pests out while allowing fresh air to circulate. MBORMC manufactures custom fly screen doors for residential kitchens, restaurant patios, commercial food service, and any doorway where flies are a problem. In this guide, we’ll explain the best mesh types for fly exclusion, style options, and how to install a fly screen door yourself.

Why You Need a Fly Screen Door

Flies are attracted to food, garbage, and organic matter. In kitchens, dining areas, and patios, they can land on food and spread pathogens like Salmonella and E. coli. Wasps can sting, posing a risk to allergic individuals. A fly screen door allows you to keep your door open for ventilation and easy access while providing a physical barrier. In commercial settings, health codes often require fly screens on doors serving food preparation areas. For homes, a fly screen door lets you enjoy a breeze without inviting a swarm into your kitchen.

Types of Fly Screen Doors from MBORMC

  • Fixed frame fly screen doors: A rigid screen mounted directly to the door frame. Most durable and provides a tight seal. Ideal for kitchen doors that are opened frequently.
  • Retractable (roll‑up) fly screen doors: The screen rolls into a slim cassette at the side. When not needed, the screen disappears completely. Perfect for patio doors and front doors.
  • Folding (pleated) fly screen doors: The mesh folds like an accordion and stacks at one or both sides. Great for wide openings like French doors.
  • Sliding fly screen doors: A rigid or folding screen that slides on a track. Commonly used for sliding patio doors.
  • Magnetic fly screen doors: A flexible screen with magnetic strips that snap shut after you walk through. No drilling or tools – ideal for renters or temporary use.

Mesh Material for Fly Screens

Flies are larger than mosquitoes, so a coarser mesh can be used without sacrificing protection. MBORMC offers two mesh options for fly screen doors:

  • PET fiber mesh (16×14 or 18×16 density): Lightweight, affordable, and offers over 85% openness for excellent visibility and airflow. UV‑resistant, rust‑proof, lasts 5–10 years. Ideal for most residential applications.
  • 304 stainless steel mesh (18×16): Heavy‑duty, pet‑proof (resists scratching), and lasts 10–20 years. Recommended for high‑traffic doors or homes with dogs.

Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black. Darker mesh reduces glare and is less visible from the outside.

Choosing the Right Fly Screen Door for Your Door Type

  • Single hinged entry doors: Retractable roll‑up screens are the most popular. Mount the cassette at the side, and the screen pulls across the doorway.
  • Sliding patio doors: Use a sliding screen door (rigid frame on a track) or a side‑mount retractable screen.
  • French doors (double hinged): A bi‑parting folding screen (two screens meeting in the middle) is best.
  • Commercial kitchen doors: Heavy‑duty stainless steel fixed frame screens are recommended.

DIY Installation – No Professional Needed

Installing a fly screen door is a straightforward DIY project. Steps vary by type:

  • Fixed frame screens: Place the screen into the door frame and secure with screws or spring clips. Takes 10–15 minutes.
  • Retractable screens: Mount the cassette using screws (wall anchors provided), attach the guide rails, and snap the screen in place. Takes 30–45 minutes.
  • Folding screens: Install the top and bottom tracks, then clip the screen assembly onto the rails. Takes 30–60 minutes.
  • Sliding screens: Install the track, then slide the screen panel into place. Takes 20–30 minutes.
  • Magnetic screens: Clean the frame, apply the self‑adhesive magnetic tape, then press the screen onto the tape. Takes 5 minutes.

All hardware is included. Basic tools like a drill and screwdriver may be needed for some types.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will a fly screen door block wasps and bees?
A: Yes. 16×14 or 18×16 mesh blocks most wasps and bees. For smaller species (like small sweat bees), 18×16 is recommended.

Q: How do I clean a fly screen door?
A: Vacuum with a brush attachment, or wipe with a damp cloth. Removable screens can be rinsed with mild soap and water.

Q: Can a fly screen door be installed on a door that opens outward?
A: Yes. For outward‑opening doors, the screen must be mounted on the interior side. Ensure the door swing does not hit the screen.

Q: Do you offer screens for commercial kitchens?
A: Yes. We provide heavy‑duty stainless steel screens for commercial kitchens, including self‑closing magnetic screens for swinging doors.
