Mosquito Screen for Office Window – Stay Productive, Bug‑Free

Published on May 6, 2026 | By MBORMC Team
Office Window Screen Window Screen Commercial Screen Insect Screen Window Custom Screen

An office should be a place of focus and productivity. But buzzing flies, crawling ants, and biting mosquitoes can quickly derail concentration and create an unprofessional environment. A mosquito screen for office window keeps pests out while allowing fresh air and natural light to enter. MBORMC offers custom screens for office windows of all sizes – from small casement windows to large storefront display windows. In this guide, we’ll discuss the best screen types for offices, how to maintain a professional appearance, and installation options.

Why Offices Need Mosquito Screens

Offices with open windows are vulnerable to insect intrusion. Flies can land on desks and contaminate paperwork; mosquitoes can bite employees; wasps can cause panic. Moreover, clients and visitors expect a pest‑free environment. Screens are a cost‑effective way to improve employee comfort and corporate image. They also reduce reliance on air conditioning, cutting energy costs.

Best Screen Types for Office Windows

  • Fixed Frame Screens – The most common choice for offices. Rigid aluminum frames provide a clean, professional look. They can be powder‑coated to match window frames. Durable and low‑maintenance.
  • Retractable Roll‑Up Screens – For conference rooms or executive offices where a clean, unobstructed view is desired, retractable screens roll up into a slim cassette at the top. When not in use, they disappear completely.
  • Sliding Screens – For large sliding or bi‑fold display windows, sliding screens on tracks offer easy operation.

Mesh Material for Office Windows

For offices, PET fiber mesh (18×16) is the standard choice. It offers excellent transparency (85% openness), preserving the view. PET is affordable, UV‑resistant, and easy to clean. For ground‑floor offices in high‑crime areas, 304 stainless steel mesh provides added security – it’s much harder to cut. Stainless steel is also fire‑resistant, meeting many commercial building codes. Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black. Dark mesh reduces glare on computer screens.

Professional Appearance and Colour Matching

We can powder‑coat frames in any RAL colour to match your existing window frames. Standard colours include white, grey, black, and bronze. The mesh itself is available in grey, dark grey, or black. Black mesh is less visible from outside and provides the cleanest look. For a truly invisible appearance, choose a retractable screen with a dark grey or black mesh.

How to Measure for Office Window Screens

  • Measure the inside width and height of the window opening at three points each. Use the smallest measurement.
  • For storefront windows, also note the frame depth – we may need to supply surface‑mount frames if there is no recess.
  • For large windows, we can supply screens in multiple panels.

Installation – DIY or Professional

For small offices, DIY installation is feasible. Our screens come with detailed instructions and all necessary hardware. For larger commercial orders, we recommend professional installation to ensure perfect alignment and compliance with local codes. We can refer local installers in many cities.

Maintenance and Cleaning

Office window screens should be cleaned quarterly to maintain appearance. Vacuum with a brush attachment or wipe with a damp cloth. For stubborn dust, remove the screen (fixed frames with clips come off easily) and rinse with mild soap and water. Stainless steel mesh can be scrubbed with a soft brush.

Building Code Compliance

Our screens meet standard building code requirements for insect exclusion. For fire escape windows, we can supply screens that are easily removable from the inside without tools – just pull a release tab. Contact us for code‑compliant options.
