Mosquito Screen for Apartment – Renters' Best Friend for Bug‑Free Living

Apartment living often means sharing walls, elevators, and unfortunately – insects. Mosquitoes can fly up from ground floors, through hallways, and into your unit. A mosquito screen for apartment is the perfect solution for renters and owners alike. MBORMC offers a range of screens that require no drilling, making them ideal for rental properties. In this guide, we’ll cover the best screen types for apartments, how to install them without damaging the frame, and how to measure for a perfect fit.

Why Apartment Dwellers Need Mosquito Screens

Apartments, especially those on lower floors or near courtyards, are prone to insect intrusion. Open windows are essential for ventilation, but without screens, you invite mosquitoes, flies, and other pests inside. Many apartment buildings restrict modifications like drilling into window frames. That’s why no‑drill screens are a game‑changer for renters. A screen also keeps out dust, pollen, and city smog, improving indoor air quality.

Best No‑Drill Screen Options for Apartments

  • Magnetic Mosquito Screen – The #1 choice for renters. The screen attaches to the window or door frame using self‑adhesive magnetic tape. No tools, no drilling, no damage. It can be removed when you move out. Works on metal frames (direct stick) or non‑metal frames (with included magnetic tape).
  • Velcro (Hook‑and‑Loop) Screen – Similar to magnetic but uses Velcro tape. Equally no‑drill and removable. Slightly less expensive.
  • Spring‑Tension Screens – A frame that pressure‑fits into the window opening using springs. No screws or tape. Good for windows that are opened rarely, as the screen can be removed when needed.
  • Retractable Screens (with adhesive mounting option) – Some retractable screens can be mounted using heavy‑duty adhesive tape instead of screws. Confirm with our sales team for availability.

Mesh Material for Apartment Screens

For apartments, PET fiber mesh is the most practical choice. It’s lightweight, affordable, and offers excellent visibility (85% openness). PET is UV‑resistant and rust‑proof. It comes in grey, dark grey, or black. Darker mesh reduces glare and is less noticeable from outside – a plus if your apartment has a view. Avoid heavy stainless steel mesh for adhesive/magnetic screens, as the weight may cause sagging.

How to Measure for an Apartment Window or Door Screen

  • Measure the width and height of the window opening (inside the frame). For magnetic/Velcro screens, measure the clear opening where the tape will be applied.
  • If your window has a handle that protrudes, note its location – we can cut a notch in the frame or use a flexible magnetic screen that goes around it.
  • For sliding balcony doors, measure the fixed panel opening (the side that does not slide).

DIY Installation – No Tools Required

Installing a magnetic or Velcro screen takes less than 5 minutes:

  1. Clean the window frame thoroughly with rubbing alcohol or a mild detergent.
  2. Apply the self‑adhesive magnetic tape (or Velcro tape) around the perimeter of the frame, following the instructions.
  3. Peel the backing from the tape on the screen (or simply press the screen onto the tape).
  4. For magnetic screens, the magnets will snap into place. For Velcro, press firmly.

No drilling, no damage. When you move out, simply peel off the tape – it leaves no residue (use a hairdryer to warm the adhesive for easier removal).

Pet‑Friendly Options for Apartment Dwellers

If you have a cat that likes to scratch screens, a standard PET mesh may tear. For pet owners, we recommend 304 stainless steel mesh (heavier, but may require a frame instead of magnetic tape). Or, consider a spring‑tension screen with stainless steel mesh that pressure‑fits into the window. Contact us for advice.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will a magnetic screen fall off?
A: Our magnets are strong enough to hold the screen in place during normal use and wind. For very windy apartments, we can provide extra‑strength magnets.

Q: Can I leave the screen up all year?
A: Yes, but prolonged exposure to direct sunlight may weaken the adhesive over time (2‑3 years). You can replace the tape when needed.

Q: Can I install a screen on a window that opens outward?
A: Yes. Apply the magnetic tape to the interior frame. The screen will be on the inside, not interfering with the window swing.

Q: What do I do with the screen when I move out?
A> Remove it by peeling off the tape. Use a hairdryer to soften the adhesive, then clean any residue with rubbing alcohol.
