Doors come in many styles – single hinged, double French, sliding patio, and more. A fixed screen door works for some, but for wide or multi‑panel doors, a pleated screen door (accordion‑style folding screen) is often the best solution. MBORMC manufactures custom pleated screen doors for openings up to 4 meters wide, with smooth gliding and a compact fold stack. In this guide, we’ll explain why pleated screen doors excel for French doors, sliding doors, and commercial entrances, and how you can install one yourself.

What Is a Pleated Screen Door?

A pleated screen door uses a concertina‑folded mesh that expands horizontally to cover the doorway and collapses into a narrow stack (typically 5–10 cm) at one or both sides when open. It rides on a top rail and may have a bottom track or a tensioned bottom edge. The screen is operated by a pull handle, and it can be locked in place with a latch. Pleated screens are especially well‑suited for double doors (French doors) because you can install one screen per door or a bi‑parting system that meets in the middle.

Advantages of Pleated Screen Doors

  • Wide coverage: Single pleated screen can cover up to 4 meters wide – far more than retractable cassettes (which max out at ~1.5m).
  • French door compatibility: Use two separate screens (one per door leaf) or a bi‑parting system.
  • No bulky cassette: The folded stack is slim and can be positioned at the side, top, or bottom.
  • Smooth operation: Glides on rails, requiring less force than spring‑loaded retractable screens.
  • Customizable stacking: Choose left stack, right stack, or split in the middle (bi‑parting).

Types of Pleated Screen Doors from MBORMC

  • Single‑leaf sliding pleated screen: For single hinged doors or one side of a French door. Stack on left or right.
  • Bi‑parting (dual) pleated screen: Two screens that meet in the middle. Ideal for double French doors.
  • Top‑mount only (no floor track): Uses a weighted bottom or tension wire – good for thresholds that cannot have a track.
  • Commercial heavy‑duty: Stainless steel mesh with reinforced rails for high‑traffic areas.

Mesh Material Options

MBORMC pleated screen doors are available with PET fiber mesh (lightweight, UV‑resistant, 18×16 density) – standard for residential. For homes with pets or heavy use, we recommend 304 stainless steel mesh – extremely strong, tear‑resistant, and pet‑proof. Both materials come in grey, dark grey, or black. For French doors that are used frequently, stainless steel offers superior longevity.

DIY Installation – Step by Step

Installing a pleated screen door is a DIY‑friendly project. Here’s what you can expect:

  • Measure each door leaf’s width and height (for double doors).
  • Decide stacking direction – left, right, or bi‑parting.
  • Order the custom pleated screen door(s) from MBORMC with your exact dimensions.
  • Install the top rail (and bottom track if applicable) using screws. Ensure level.
  • Clip the screen assembly into the rail.
  • Attach the pull handle and install the latch at the opposite side (or center for bi‑parting).
  • Test sliding – it should move smoothly without binding.

Basic tools required: drill, screwdriver, level, measuring tape. Total time: 30–60 minutes per door.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I install a pleated screen door on a door that opens outward?
A: Yes. The screen is mounted on the interior side. Ensure the door swing does not hit the screen – the screen should be installed on the opposite side of the hinges.

Q: How do I clean a pleated screen door?
A: Use a soft brush or vacuum with a brush attachment. Do not machine wash. For stubborn dirt, wipe with a damp cloth.

Q: Will the pleats wear out over time?
A: PET mesh pleats may relax after 5–8 years; stainless steel pleats last much longer. Replacement mesh can be ordered.

Q: Can I use a pleated screen door without a bottom track?
A: Yes, we offer a “trackless” option with a weighted bottom hem or tension wire. This is ideal for thresholds that cannot have a track.
