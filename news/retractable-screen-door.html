Retractable Screen Door – Convenient, Hands‑Free Access with Full Insect Protection

A screen door is essential for keeping insects out while allowing fresh air to flow. But traditional fixed screen doors can be bulky and interfere with the aesthetics of your home. A retractable screen door offers the perfect solution – it rolls away when not needed, giving you an unobstructed opening. MBORMC manufactures custom retractable screen doors for all types of entryways: single hinged doors, sliding patio doors, French doors, and commercial entrances. In this guide, we’ll cover how retractable screen doors work, their benefits, installation, and why MBORMC is your best choice.

What Is a Retractable Screen Door?

A retractable screen door consists of a mesh screen attached to a spring‑loaded cassette. The cassette can be mounted vertically (at the side of the door) or horizontally (above the door). When you need insect protection, you pull the screen across the opening and latch it at the opposite side. When not needed, you release the latch and the screen automatically retracts into the cassette. The cassette is compact (typically 2 inches wide) and can be colour‑matched to your door frame, making it nearly invisible when retracted.

Advantages Over Traditional Screen Doors

  • Unobstructed view: No permanent screen blocking your sightline.
  • Full door access: You can carry large items through without snagging on a screen.
  • Aesthetic: The cassette blends with the frame, and the screen disappears when not in use.
  • Ideal for sliding doors: Traditional sliding screen doors can be heavy and derail; retractable screens are lightweight.
  • Rental‑friendly: Minimal drilling required (screw holes can be filled later).

Types of Retractable Screen Doors from MBORMC

  • Single‑door vertical cassette: Mounted at the side, pulls horizontally. Perfect for hinged front doors, back doors, and French doors (one per leaf).
  • Sliding door horizontal cassette: Mounted at the top, pulls down. Or side‑mounted, pulls across – our engineers will advise based on your door style.
  • Bi‑parting system: Two cassettes mounted on both sides, meeting in the middle. For extra‑wide openings (e.g., double patio doors).
  • Hands‑free magnetic latch: For sliding doors, the screen can be equipped with a magnetic catch that engages automatically when you slide the door closed.

Mesh Material Options

MBORMC retractable screen doors are available with PET fiber mesh (lightweight, UV‑resistant, 18×16 density) – ideal for most residential applications. For high‑traffic areas or homes with pets, we recommend 304 stainless steel mesh – extremely strong, tear‑resistant, and pet‑proof. Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black.

DIY Installation – Step by Step

Installing a retractable screen door is a straightforward DIY project. Here’s what you can expect:

  • Measure your door opening width and height. For sliding doors, also note if it’s a left‑ or right‑hand sliding.
  • Order the custom screen door from MBORMC with your exact dimensions.
  • Mount the cassette using screws (wall anchors provided). Use a level to ensure it’s straight.
  • Attach the guide rails (for side‑mount) or track (for top‑mount).
  • Slide the mesh into the rails, attach the pull handle, and install the latch at the opposite side.
  • Test the operation – it should pull smoothly and retract completely.

Basic tools required: drill, screwdriver, level, measuring tape. Total time: 30–60 minutes depending on complexity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I install a retractable screen door on a door that opens outward?
A: Yes. For outward‑opening doors, choose a top‑mount cassette (pulls down) or a side‑mount cassette that pulls across, ensuring the screen does not interfere with the door swing.

Q: How do I clean the screen?
A: Use a soft brush or vacuum with a brush attachment. For deeper cleaning, unclip the screen (most models allow removal) and rinse with mild soap and water.

Q: Are retractable screen doors secure against pets?
A: PET mesh will not resist scratching. We strongly recommend upgrading to 304 stainless steel mesh for pet owners. The metal mesh is virtually bite‑proof.

Q: What’s the maximum width for a single retractable screen door?
A: For smooth operation, we recommend up to 1.2m width for a single cassette. For wider openings, use a bi‑parting system.
