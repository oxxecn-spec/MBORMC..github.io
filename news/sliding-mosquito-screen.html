Sliding Mosquito Screen – Perfect for Horizontal Sliding Windows & Doors

Published on May 2, 2026 | By MBORMC Team

If you have horizontal sliding windows or sliding patio doors, a sliding mosquito screen is the most natural and effective solution. Unlike fixed screens that block the view or retractable screens that require a cassette, a sliding screen uses a simple track system and glides parallel to your window or door. MBORMC manufactures custom sliding mosquito screens that fit perfectly into existing sliding window frames or mount as an add‑on for sliding doors. In this guide, we’ll explain the benefits of sliding screens, installation, and why they are ideal for modern homes.

What Is a Sliding Mosquito Screen?

A sliding mosquito screen is a mesh panel mounted on a track that allows it to slide horizontally (or vertically) to cover an opening. It typically consists of a rigid aluminum frame (or a flexible PVC frame) that holds the mesh, plus a top and bottom track. For sliding windows, the screen is often installed on the interior side and slides in the same direction as the window sash. For sliding doors, the screen slides on its own independent track, allowing the door to move freely behind or in front of it.

Advantages of Sliding Mosquito Screens

  • Perfect for sliding windows: The screen moves with the same motion as the window – just slide it open when you want fresh air.
  • No view obstruction: When fully open, the screen stacks to one side, leaving the window completely clear.
  • Simple, durable mechanism: No springs or complex parts – just smooth‑gliding rollers.
  • Cost‑effective: Simpler than retractable systems, yet very functional.
  • Can be installed on both single and double sliders.

Types of Sliding Screens from MBORMC

  • Interior sliding window screen: Fits inside the window frame, slides horizontally. Common for vinyl/aluminium sliding windows.
  • Exterior sliding screen: Mounts on the outside for windows that open inward. Less common but available.
  • Sliding door screen: A heavy‑duty version with a reinforced frame and larger rollers. Can be made as a single panel (slides to one side) or bi‑parting (two panels meet in the middle).

Mesh Material Options

We offer sliding screens with PET fiber mesh (lightweight, UV‑resistant, 18×16 density) – standard for residential use. For high‑traffic sliding doors or homes with pets, we recommend 304 stainless steel mesh for extra durability. Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black. For sliding windows, PET is usually sufficient. For sliding doors that see daily use, stainless steel offers longer life.

DIY Installation – Step by Step

Installing a sliding mosquito screen is straightforward. Here’s the process:

  • Measure the width and height of your sliding window or door opening.
  • Order the custom screen from MBORMC with your exact dimensions, specifying whether it’s for a window or door.
  • For windows: Remove the existing track (if any) or attach the provided track using screws or double‑sided tape. Slide the screen into the track.
  • For doors: Install the top and bottom tracks on the interior side aligned with the door. Slide the screen panel into the tracks.
  • Install a handle on the screen’s moving edge, and optional latches to keep it closed.
  • Test sliding – adjust track alignment if necessary.

Basic tools required: drill, screwdriver, level, measuring tape. Total time: 20–40 minutes per screen.

Custom Sizing – Perfect Fit

MBORMC manufactures each sliding screen to your exact measurements. You provide width and height (in cm or inches). We cut the frame and mesh precisely, and supply tracks cut to length. This ensures a gap‑free fit – critical for keeping out insects. Maximum width: up to 3 meters for a single sliding screen (heavier above that). For larger openings, consider a bi‑parting system.

Where to Use Sliding Screens

  • Sliding windows (horizontal): The most common application – found in many modern apartments and homes.
  • Sliding patio doors: A heavy‑duty sliding screen is a classic solution.
  • Vertical sliding windows (double‑hung): Although vertical sliding windows exist, our sliding screens are primarily horizontal; for vertical, consider a roll‑up or fixed screen.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I install a sliding screen on a window that already has a screen?
A: If the existing screen is damaged or missing, replace it with our custom screen. If you want a second screen (e.g., for a double slider), measure the window carefully – we can supply a screen for the fixed panel side.

Q: How do I clean a sliding screen?
A: Vacuum the mesh with a brush attachment. You can also remove the screen from the tracks (lift it out) and rinse with mild soap and water.

Q: Is a sliding screen secure against pets?
A: Standard PET mesh can be scratched. Use 304 stainless steel mesh for pet‑proof protection.

Q: Can a sliding screen be installed on the outside of a sliding window?
A: Yes, but exterior installation may expose the screen to weather. Our frames are UV‑resistant, but interior mounting is recommended for longevity.
