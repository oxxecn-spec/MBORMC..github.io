Retractable Mosquito Screen – Convenient, Space-Saving Insect Protection

Published on May 1, 2026 | By MBORMC Team

A fixed screen can be an eyesore when you want an unobstructed view. A retractable mosquito screen offers the perfect balance – protection when you need it, invisible when you don’t. MBORMC’s custom retractable screens roll up into a slim cassette at the top or side of your window or door, disappearing completely when not in use. In this guide, we’ll explain how retractable screens work, their advantages over fixed screens, and how you can install one yourself in less than an hour.

What Is a Retractable Mosquito Screen?

A retractable mosquito screen consists of a spring‑loaded mesh that rolls into a compact cassette. The cassette can be mounted horizontally (above a window or door) or vertically (at the side). When you need protection, you pull the screen down (or across) using a handle, and it locks into place at the opposite side. When not needed, you release the latch, and the screen automatically retracts back into the cassette. The entire mechanism is hidden inside the cassette, leaving a clean, minimalist appearance.

Advantages Over Fixed Screens

  • Unobstructed view: When retracted, you have a completely clear window or door opening.
  • Easy access: Perfect for doors – you can walk through without pushing past a screen.
  • Aesthetic: The cassette is discreet and can be colour‑matched to your frame.
  • Durability: The mesh is protected inside the cassette when not in use, extending its lifespan.
  • Ideal for rental properties: No permanent modification if you choose a screw‑mount cassette (still leaves small holes, but can be filled later).

Types of Retractable Screens from MBORMC

  • Vertical roll‑up (top‑mount): Pulls down from the top. Ideal for windows, French doors, and single doors.
  • Horizontal roll‑up (side‑mount): Pulls across from the side. Best for sliding doors, wide windows, and patio entrances.
  • Bi‑parting (two cassettes): Two screens that meet in the middle. For very wide openings (up to 4 meters).

All types can be operated with a simple pull handle. Some versions include a magnetic latch for a tight seal.

Mesh Material Options

MBORMC retractable screens are available with PET fiber mesh (lightweight, UV‑resistant, 18×16 or 20×20 density) or 304 stainless steel mesh (heavy‑duty, pet‑proof, lasts 10–20 years). For standard residential use, PET is sufficient. For homes with dogs or high traffic, stainless steel is recommended. Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black.

DIY Installation – Step by Step

Installing a retractable mosquito screen is a straightforward DIY project. Here’s the general process (detailed instructions included with each order):

  • Measure your window or door opening width and height.
  • Order the custom screen from MBORMC with your exact dimensions.
  • Mount the cassette using screws (wall anchors provided). Use a level to ensure it’s straight.
  • Attach the guide rails (for side‑mount) or bottom track (for top‑mount).
  • Clip the mesh into the rails and attach the pull handle.
  • Test the operation – it should roll smoothly and latch securely.

Basic tools required: drill, screwdriver, level, measuring tape. Total time: 30–45 minutes per screen.

Custom Sizing – Perfect Fit

Unlike off‑the‑shelf retractable screens that come in fixed widths, MBORMC manufactures each screen to your exact dimensions. You provide the width and height (in cm or inches), and we cut the cassette, rails, and mesh precisely. This ensures a gap‑free fit – critical for keeping out small insects. We support widths up to 3 meters for single cassettes and up to 4 meters for bi‑parting systems.

Where to Use Retractable Screens

  • Kitchen windows: Retract when cooking, deploy when you want fresh air without flies.
  • Patio sliding doors: The most popular application – enjoy bug‑free evenings with the door open.
  • Balcony doors: Perfect for apartments where space is tight.
  • French doors: Use a bi‑parting system for double doors.
  • Commercial entrances: Restaurants and cafes can install retractable screens that are deployed only during open hours.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does the spring mechanism last?
A: Our springs are rated for over 10,000 cycles, equivalent to daily use for 27 years.

Q: Can I install a retractable screen on a window that opens outward?
A: Yes. For outward‑opening windows, choose a side‑mount cassette that pulls across horizontally, not a top‑mount that pulls down.

Q: Do retractable screens keep out no‑see‑ums?
A: With our 20×20 mesh upgrade, yes. Standard 18×16 mesh blocks most, but fine midges may pass. Upgrade to 20×20 for full protection.
