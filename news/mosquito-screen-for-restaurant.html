Mosquito Screen for Restaurant – Commercial‑Grade Insect Protection for Food Safety

Published on May 5, 2026 | By MBORMC Team
Restaurant Mosquito Screen Commercial Screen Heavy Duty Screen Retractable Screen Door Cafe Screen

For restaurants and cafes, flies and other insects are not just a nuisance – they are a health code violation waiting to happen. A mosquito screen for restaurant provides a professional, durable barrier that keeps insects out of your kitchen, dining area, and patio while maintaining airflow and visibility. MBORMC manufactures commercial‑grade screens using heavy‑duty materials designed for high‑traffic food service environments. In this guide, we’ll cover the best screen types for restaurants, how to meet health department requirements, and installation options.

Why Restaurants Need Professional Mosquito Screens

Flies can carry bacteria like Salmonella and E. coli, contaminating food and preparation surfaces. Health inspectors routinely check for proper insect exclusion. A missing or torn screen can lead to citations, fines, or even closure. Moreover, customers expect a pest‑free dining experience, especially on outdoor patios. Investing in high‑quality screens protects your reputation, your customers, and your business. MBORMC screens are made from 304 stainless steel mesh (standard for commercial use) and reinforced aluminum frames to withstand constant opening and closing.

Best Screen Types for Restaurant Openings

  • Sliding Screen Doors – Ideal for back‑of‑house doors leading to loading docks or outdoor trash areas. Heavy‑duty sliding screens on a track are durable and easy to operate.
  • Retractable Roll‑Up Screens – Perfect for patio entrances and kitchen doors that are opened frequently. The screen disappears when not needed, preserving aesthetics.
  • Fixed Frame Screens – For kitchen windows that are always open for ventilation, a fixed frame screen with stainless steel mesh is the most durable option.
  • Folding (Pleated) Screens – For wide patio openings (e.g., large folding glass walls), pleated screens stack at the sides and can be custom‑coloured to match the restaurant’s décor.
  • Magnetic Strip Curtains – For walk‑in coolers or freezer doors, we can supply clear PVC strip curtains that also block insects (not mesh, but effective for temperature‑controlled areas).

Mesh Material – 304 Stainless Steel is the Only Choice for Commercial Kitchens

For restaurants, we strongly recommend 304 stainless steel mesh (18×16 density). PET fiber mesh is not fire‑resistant and can be torn by delivery staff or damaged by grease. Stainless steel is:

  • Fire‑resistant – important for kitchen safety.
  • Easy to clean – can be pressure washed or scrubbed.
  • Pet‑proof and impact‑resistant – withstands accidental kicks and carts.
  • Corrosion‑resistant – ideal for outdoor patios and coastal locations.

The mesh is available in dark grey or black – black reduces glare and is less visible from the outside, maintaining a clean appearance.

Health Code Compliance

Local health codes typically require that screens for food service areas have mesh no larger than 1.2mm (16×16 mesh). Our 18×16 and 20×20 meshes exceed this requirement. Screens must also be durable, non‑absorbent, and easy to clean. Our aluminum frames and stainless steel mesh meet all criteria. We provide documentation upon request to help with inspections.

Custom Sizing for Commercial Openings

We manufacture screens for any size opening, from small kitchen windows to large loading dock doors. Maximum dimensions: 4m wide for folding screens, 3m for sliding screens, and 3m high. For extra‑large openings, we can design multi‑panel systems or motorized retractable screens. Provide your measurements in cm or inches, and we will produce a screen that fits perfectly.

Installation – DIY or Professional

For restaurant owners, we recommend professional installation to ensure perfect alignment and compliance. We can refer local contractors in many cities. However, our screens are designed for easy DIY installation if you have maintenance staff available. All hardware and detailed instructions are included. For multi‑screen projects, we offer on‑site installation training.
