What Is a Sliding Screen Door?

A sliding screen door is a mesh panel mounted on a track that slides horizontally to open or close. It typically consists of a rigid aluminum frame, a mesh screen, top and bottom tracks, rollers, and a handle. Unlike fixed screens, a sliding screen door can be opened when you need to pass through, and closed when you want insect protection. It is designed to operate independently of the main sliding door, meaning you can slide the screen door while the glass door stays open or closed.

Mesh Material Options

We offer two mesh options. PET fiber mesh (18×16) is lightweight, UV‑resistant, and affordable – perfect for standard residential use. 304 stainless steel mesh is extremely strong, tear‑resistant, and pet‑proof. For homes with dogs or cats, stainless steel is the best choice. Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black. Dark grey/black mesh offers better outward visibility and reduces glare.

How to Measure for a Sliding Screen Door

Accurate measurement is critical for a gap‑free fit. Follow these steps:

  • Measure the width of the door opening from inside the frame (left jamb to right jamb) at top, middle, and bottom. Use the smallest measurement.
  • Measure the height from the top of the track (or frame) to the floor at left, center, and right. Use the smallest measurement.
  • If your door has a threshold, measure to the top of the threshold.
  • Provide both width and height in cm or inches. MBORMC will subtract a small clearance (2‑3mm) to ensure smooth sliding.

DIY Installation – Step by Step

Installing a sliding screen door is straightforward:

  • Order your custom screen from MBORMC with your measurements.
  • Remove any existing old screen door (if present).
  • Clean the track area thoroughly.
  • If a new bottom track is needed, install it using screws (provided) or adhesive tape (for no‑drill option).
  • Install the top track (usually the screen frame itself hooks into a top guide).
  • Place the screen door into the top track, then lower it into the bottom track.
  • Adjust the roller tension screws to level the screen and ensure smooth sliding.
  • Install the handle (included).

Basic tools required: drill, screwdriver, level, measuring tape. Total time: 30–60 minutes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I install a sliding screen door on a door that opens outward?
A: Sliding screen doors are designed for sliding doors, not hinged outward‑opening doors. For outward‑opening doors, consider a retractable or pleated screen.

Q: How do I clean my sliding screen door?
A: Vacuum the mesh with a brush attachment. For deeper cleaning, you can lift the screen out of the track and rinse with mild soap and water.

Q: Is the screen secure against pets?
A: PET mesh can be scratched. Upgrade to 304 stainless steel mesh for pet‑proof protection.

Q: How long does the screen last?
A: PET mesh: 5–10 years; stainless steel: 10–20 years. The aluminum frame lasts decades.
