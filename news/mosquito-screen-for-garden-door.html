Mosquito Screen for Garden Door – Let Nature In, Keep Bugs Out

Published on May 5, 2026 | By MBORMC Team
Garden Door Screen French Door Screen Sliding Door Screen Retractable Screen Pet Proof Screen

A garden door is your direct connection to nature – fresh air, birdsong, and the beauty of your backyard. Unfortunately, it’s also an open invitation for mosquitoes, flies, and other insects. A mosquito screen for garden door lets you keep the door open for hours without inviting pests inside. MBORMC offers custom screens for all types of garden doors – French doors, sliding glass doors, single hinged doors, and even stable doors. In this guide, we’ll explore the best screen styles for garden doors, how to choose pet‑friendly materials, and simple installation tips.

Why Your Garden Door Needs a Mosquito Screen

Garden doors are often left open while you enjoy a morning coffee, tend to plants, or let pets run in and out. Without a screen, you’re sharing your home with every mosquito in the neighborhood. A screen allows you to:

  • Ventilate your home without worry.
  • Enjoy the sound of birds and the scent of flowers without the buzz of insects.
  • Let children and pets play safely between indoors and outdoors.
  • Reduce reliance on air conditioning during mild weather.

Best Screen Types for Garden Doors

The ideal screen depends on your garden door style and how often you use it.

  • Sliding Screen Door – Perfect for sliding glass garden doors. A rigid frame screen slides on a track, just like the glass door. Durable and classic.
  • Retractable Roll‑Up Screen – For a clean, invisible look. The screen rolls into a slim cassette at the side. When not needed, it disappears completely. Great for single‑hinged or sliding doors.
  • Folding (Pleated) Screen for French Doors – For double doors that open outward, a bi‑parting folding screen stacks on both sides and meets in the middle. Each door can open independently.
  • Magnetic Mesh Curtain – For renters or temporary use, a magnetic curtain attaches with tape. Walk through and it snaps shut. No drilling, no tools.

Mesh Material: PET vs. 304 Stainless Steel

For garden doors, PET fiber mesh (18×16) is often sufficient – it’s lightweight, has excellent visibility, and resists UV rays. However, if you have pets that may scratch or children who might push against the screen, consider 304 stainless steel mesh. Stainless steel is pet‑proof, tear‑resistant, and ideal for high‑traffic areas. Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black. Dark mesh reduces glare and is less visible from the garden side.

Pet‑Friendly Features for Garden Doors

If your dog or cat likes to go in and out, consider adding a pet flap to your screen door. We can cut a small hinged door into a fixed sliding screen or a retractable screen panel, allowing your pet to pass through without damaging the mesh. For magnetic curtains, pets can simply push through – the magnets will re‑seal behind them. For maximum durability, choose 304 stainless steel mesh to resist scratching.

How to Measure for a Garden Door Screen

  • For sliding doors: measure the width of the fixed panel (the side that does not slide) and the height from the top track to the floor.
  • For French doors: measure the total width of both doors when closed, and the height. For bi‑parting screens, we will divide the width by two.
  • For single hinged doors: measure the clear door opening (inside the frame).
  • If your garden door has a high threshold, consider a screen with a bottom track or a weighted magnetic curtain.

DIY Installation – Simple Weekend Project

Installing a garden door screen is straightforward. For sliding screens, install the top and bottom tracks using screws (provided), then slide the panel in. For retractable screens, mount the cassette, attach the guide rails, and snap the screen in place. For magnetic curtains, just clean the frame and apply the adhesive magnetic tape – no tools needed. All hardware is included. Basic tools (drill, screwdriver, level) may be required for sliding or retractable screens.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I install a screen on a garden door that opens outward?
A: Yes. The screen must be mounted on the interior side. Retractable or sliding screens work well.

Q: Will a screen block my view of the garden?
A: Our high‑transparency PET mesh has over 85% openness. You’ll barely notice it. Stainless steel offers about 75% openness – still very clear.

Q: How do I clean a garden door screen?
A: Vacuum with a brush attachment. Removable screens can be rinsed with mild soap and water.

Q: Can I get a screen with a pet door?
A: Yes, we can add a pet flap to fixed sliding screens or retractable screens. Contact us for details.
