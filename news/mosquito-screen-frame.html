Mosquito Screen Frame – The Backbone of Long‑Lasting Insect Protection

Published on May 4, 2026 | By MBORMC Team
Mosquito Screen Frame Aluminum Mosquito Screen Custom Mosquito Screen Mosquito Screen System Heavy Duty Mosquito Screen

The frame is just as critical as the mesh when it comes to durability, fit, and aesthetics of your mosquito screen. A mosquito screen frame must be strong enough to resist bending, lightweight enough for easy handling, and corrosion‑resistant for long life. MBORMC uses premium 6063‑T5 aluminum alloy for all our frames – the same grade used in architectural windows and doors. In this guide, we’ll explain why aluminum is the superior choice, the different frame profiles available, how to choose the right frame for your opening, and how to care for your frames to ensure decades of service.

Why Aluminum Frames?

  • Rust‑proof: Aluminum does not rust, making it ideal for humid coastal areas and bathrooms.
  • Lightweight: Easy to handle and install compared to steel.
  • Strong: 6063‑T5 aluminum has excellent tensile strength and resists bending.
  • Corrosion‑resistant: Powder‑coated finishes add extra protection against salt air and pollution.
  • Aesthetic: Can be painted any colour (white, grey, black, bronze, wood grain, custom RAL) to match your window/door frames.
  • Recyclable: Aluminum is 100% recyclable, eco‑friendly.

Frame Profiles Available from MBORMC

We offer several frame profiles depending on your application and desired aesthetics:

  • Standard fixed frame (25mm × 12mm): Most common for residential windows. Corners joined with pre‑formed corner keys. Mesh held by rubber spline.
  • Heavy‑duty fixed frame (30mm × 15mm): For larger windows (over 1.5m) or commercial applications. Thicker profile resists bending.
  • Roll‑formed frame for sliding screens: A continuous frame (no corners) designed to ride on a track. Used for sliding window and door screens.
  • Z‑frame (self‑storing): A frame that is slightly larger than the window opening and held in place by spring clips – easy to remove for cleaning.
  • Retractable cassette frame: The aluminum cassette housing for roll‑up screens. Usually 50mm × 50mm or 60mm × 60mm.
  • Folding (pleated) track system: Top and bottom rails made of aluminum, often 20mm high.

Colour and Finish Options

Our frames can be powder‑coated in any colour. Standard colours include:

  • White (RAL 9016)
  • Off‑white / Cream
  • Light grey (RAL 7042)
  • Dark grey (RAL 7016)
  • Black (RAL 9005)
  • Bronze (RAL 8017)
  • Wood grain (foil wrapped – extra cost)

We can also match any RAL colour for an additional fee. The powder‑coated finish is UV‑resistant, chip‑resistant, and easy to clean.

Custom Sizing and Shapes

We manufacture frames to your exact measurements. For rectangular openings, provide width and height in cm or inches. For non‑rectangular shapes (arched, circular, triangular), provide a template or detailed drawing. We can produce frames for openings up to 2.5m × 2.5m in a single piece; larger sizes may require reinforcement or multi‑panel construction.

Frame Installation Methods

  • Screw‑mount: Frames have pre‑drilled holes for screws. You attach the frame directly to the window or door frame. Most secure.
  • Clip‑mount (spring clips): Z‑frames use spring clips that hold the frame in place without screws. Easy to remove for cleaning.
  • Magnetic attachment: For light‑duty applications, the frame can have embedded magnets to stick to metal frames or magnetic tape.
  • Track mount: For sliding screens, the frame slides into top and bottom tracks.

Frame Maintenance and Care

  • Clean frames with a damp cloth and mild detergent. Avoid abrasive cleaners.
  • Inspect corner keys periodically – tighten if loose.
  • For powder‑coated finishes, avoid scratching with sharp tools.
  • If the frame becomes bent, it may need replacement; aluminum can sometimes be straightened carefully.

How to Measure for a Replacement Frame

If you already have a screen but the frame is damaged, you can order just the frame. Measure the existing frame’s outside dimensions. Provide these to MBORMC, and we’ll manufacture a new frame. You can then transfer the mesh (if still good) or order new mesh as well.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I buy just the frame without mesh?
A: Yes, we supply empty frames. You can install your own mesh or order mesh separately.

Q: What is the difference between 6063-T5 and standard aluminum?
A: 6063-T5 is a heat‑treated alloy with higher strength and better corrosion resistance than generic aluminum. It’s the industry standard for window frames.

Q: How do I join the corners of a frame?
A> Our frames come with corner keys that insert into the hollow profiles. You tap them in with a rubber mallet for a tight joint.

Q: Are your frames suitable for coastal areas?
A: Yes, with powder coating, our aluminum frames resist salt spray. We also offer marine‑grade finish for extreme conditions.
