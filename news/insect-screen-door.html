Insect Screen Door – Keep All Flying Pests Out While Letting Fresh Air In

Published on May 3, 2026 | By MBORMC Team
Insect Screen Door Mosquito Screen Door Fly Screen Door Retractable Screen Door Sliding Screen Door

Doors are the largest openings in your home and the most common entry point for flying insects. An insect screen door provides a permanent, reliable barrier against mosquitoes, flies, wasps, midges, and other pests while allowing fresh air to circulate freely. MBORMC manufactures custom insect screen doors for all door types – hinged, sliding, French, and even commercial entrances. In this guide, we’ll explore the different styles of insect screen doors, the benefits of each, and how to choose the perfect solution for your home.

Why You Need an Insect Screen Door

Without a screen, every time you open your door you invite insects inside. This is especially problematic during summer evenings, near gardens, or in areas with high mosquito populations. An insect screen door allows you to keep your door open for ventilation, natural light, and easy access without the nuisance of pests. It also helps keep out dust, pollen, and small debris, improving indoor air quality. For homes with children, elderly, or pets, a physical barrier is the safest and most effective method of insect control.

Types of Insect Screen Doors from MBORMC

We offer several door screen styles to match different door configurations and usage patterns:

  • Fixed frame screen doors: A rigid screen mounted directly to the door frame. Best for doors that are not opened frequently (e.g., side doors). They provide the tightest seal and are very durable.
  • Retractable (roll‑up) screen doors: The screen rolls into a slim cassette mounted at the side or top. When not needed, it disappears completely. Ideal for front doors and patio doors that are used often.
  • Folding (pleated) screen doors: The screen folds like an accordion and stacks at one or both sides. Perfect for wide openings (French doors) where a single cassette cannot cover the width.
  • Sliding screen doors: A rigid or folding screen that slides on a track. Commonly used for sliding patio doors. They are heavy‑duty and can be made with pet‑resistant mesh.
  • Magnetic screen doors: A flexible screen with magnetic strips that snap shut after you walk through. No drilling or tools – great for renters or temporary use.

Choosing the Right Screen for Your Door Type

  • Single hinged entry doors: Retractable roll‑up screens are the most popular. Mount the cassette at the side, and the screen pulls across the doorway.
  • Sliding patio doors: Use a sliding screen door (rigid frame on a track) or a side‑mount retractable screen. Sliding screens are classic and very durable.
  • French doors (double hinged): A bi‑parting folding screen (two screens meeting in the middle) is best. Each door can be opened independently, and the screen stacks on both sides.
  • Pet doors: We can cut a pet flap into a fixed frame screen or provide a separate small magnetic screen for the pet entrance.
  • Rental properties: Magnetic or Velcro screens require no permanent installation – just peel and stick.

Mesh Material: PET vs. 304 Stainless Steel

Choosing the right mesh material is crucial for durability and effectiveness:

  • PET fiber mesh (18×16 density): Lightweight, affordable, and offers excellent visibility (85% openness). UV‑resistant and rust‑proof. Lasts 5–10 years. Ideal for standard residential use.
  • 304 stainless steel mesh (18×16 or 20×20): Extremely strong, pet‑proof (resists scratching and biting), fire‑resistant, and lasts 10–20 years. Slightly less transparent (75% openness) but much more durable. Recommended for homes with dogs, cats, or high traffic.

Both meshes are available in grey, dark grey, or black. Darker mesh provides better outward visibility (less glare) and is less noticeable from the outside.

DIY Installation – No Professional Needed

All MBORMC insect screen doors are designed for easy DIY installation. Each order includes detailed instructions and all necessary hardware. Installation steps vary by type:

  • Fixed frame screens: Simply place the screen into the door frame and secure with screws or clips. Takes 10–15 minutes.
  • Retractable screens: Mount the cassette using screws (wall anchors provided), attach the guide rails, and snap the screen in place. Takes 30–45 minutes.
  • Folding screens: Install the top and bottom tracks, then clip the screen assembly onto the rails. Takes 30–60 minutes.
  • Sliding screens: Install the track, then slide the screen panel into place. Takes 20–30 minutes.
  • Magnetic screens: No tools – just clean the frame, apply the magnetic tape, and press the screen on. Takes 5 minutes.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can an insect screen door be installed on a door that opens outward?
A: Yes. For outward‑opening doors, choose a retractable or folding screen that mounts on the interior side. Ensure there is enough clearance for the door swing.

Q: How do I clean my insect screen door?
A: Vacuum gently with a brush attachment. For deeper cleaning, remove the screen (if removable) and rinse with mild soap and water. Fixed screens can be wiped in place.

Q: Are your screen doors secure against pets?
A: Standard PET mesh can be scratched. Upgrade to 304 stainless steel mesh for pet‑proof protection.

Q: What is the warranty?
A: All MBORMC insect screen doors come with a 2‑year warranty against manufacturing defects.
