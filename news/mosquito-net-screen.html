What Is a Mosquito Net Screen?

A mosquito net screen is a fine mesh barrier specifically designed to block mosquitoes while allowing air to circulate. Traditionally, these were hanging nets draped over beds. Today, we offer frame‑based screens that install on windows and doors – providing whole‑home protection. A mosquito net screen can be fixed, retractable, folding, or magnetic. The key is a mesh aperture small enough to prevent even the smallest mosquito species (like Aedes aegypti) from passing through – typically 18×16 threads per inch or finer.

Why Choose a Mosquito Net Screen Over Sprays or Coils

Chemical insecticides (sprays, coils, vaporizers) can have health risks, especially for children, pregnant women, and pets. They also only provide temporary protection. Electronic bug zappers kill some insects but also attract more. A physical mosquito net screen offers 24/7, chemical‑free protection with no ongoing costs. It’s the World Health Organization’s recommended first line of defense against mosquito‑borne diseases like malaria, dengue, and Zika. Plus, it allows you to keep windows open for fresh air – something screens do better than any other method.

Material Options: PET vs. 304 Stainless Steel

MBORMC uses two primary mesh materials. PET fiber mesh is lightweight, breathable, and affordable. It provides excellent mosquito protection while maintaining high transparency and airflow. PET is UV‑stabilized and won’t rust. It’s available in grey, dark grey, or black. 304 stainless steel mesh is incredibly strong, pet‑proof, and fire‑resistant. It’s ideal for ground‑floor windows or areas where pets may scratch. Stainless steel also offers higher tear resistance and longevity. For maximum mosquito protection, we recommend a mesh count of 18×16 threads per inch, which blocks all common mosquito species.

Frequently Asked Questions About Mosquito Net Screens

Q: Will a mosquito net screen block very small gnats (midges)?
A: Our standard 18×16 mesh blocks most midges. For areas with very tiny biting midges (no‑see‑ums), we recommend upgrading to 20×20 mesh. Ask us for details.

Q: Can I install a mosquito net screen on a sliding window?
A: Yes. Our retractable roll‑up and fixed frame screens work perfectly on sliding windows. For sliding windows that open horizontally, we recommend a vertical roll‑up screen that mounts at the side.

Q: How long does a PET mesh mosquito screen last?
A: Under normal use, PET mesh lasts 5–10 years. It resists UV, mold, and mildew. Stainless steel mesh lasts 10–20 years.
