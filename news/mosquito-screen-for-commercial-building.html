Mosquito Screen for Commercial Building – Professional Insect Control for Businesses

Published on May 6, 2026 | By MBORMC Team
Commercial Mosquito Screen Heavy Duty Screen Commercial Screen Industrial Insect Screen Custom Screen

Commercial buildings – offices, retail stores, warehouses, showrooms – all face insect challenges. An open door or window invites flies, mosquitoes, and other pests that can harm your brand image, contaminate products, and even violate health codes. A mosquito screen for commercial building provides a professional, durable solution that keeps insects out while maintaining ventilation and natural light. MBORMC manufactures heavy‑duty screens for commercial applications, with 304 stainless steel mesh, reinforced aluminum frames, and custom sizing for any opening. In this guide, we’ll cover the key considerations for commercial screens, types available, and how to order for large‑scale projects.

Why Commercial Buildings Need Specialized Screens

Commercial buildings have different requirements than residential properties. Screens must withstand high traffic, frequent opening and closing, and in some cases, impact from carts or equipment. Aesthetics matter – screens should look professional and blend with the building’s architecture. And for food‑related businesses, screens must comply with health department regulations. MBORMC commercial screens are built to meet these demands.

Applications for Commercial Insect Screens

  • Office buildings: Keep flies out of workspaces, improving employee comfort and productivity.
  • Retail stores: Prevent insects from landing on merchandise, especially in grocery stores, bakeries, and clothing shops.
  • Warehouses and distribution centers: Loading dock screens keep pests out while allowing ventilation.
  • Showrooms and auto dealerships: Maintain an open, inviting environment without insects.
  • Healthcare facilities (hospitals, clinics): Prevent mosquito‑borne diseases and maintain sterile environments.
  • Hotels and hospitality: Guest rooms and common areas need insect protection for comfort and reputation.

Screen Types for Commercial Buildings

  • Fixed Frame Screens – The most common for commercial windows. Rigid aluminum frame with 304 stainless steel mesh. Available in any colour to match the building exterior.
  • Sliding Screen Doors – For commercial entrances and loading docks. Heavy‑duty sliding screens with industrial‑grade rollers and tracks.
  • Retractable Roll‑Up Screens – For storefronts or areas where a clean, invisible look is desired. Motorized options available for large openings.
  • Folding (Pleated) Screens – For wide openings like restaurant patios or conference room partitions.
  • Magnetic Strip Curtains – For walk‑in coolers, freezers, or high‑traffic doorways where insect control and temperature retention are both needed.

Mesh Material – 304 Stainless Steel is the Standard

For commercial applications, 304 stainless steel mesh is the only choice. It is fire‑resistant, extremely durable, and easy to clean. PET mesh is not suitable for commercial use because it can be torn, stained, or burned. Stainless steel lasts 10‑20 years, even with daily abuse. Mesh density: 18×16 for standard protection, or 20×20 for no‑see‑um areas.

Custom Sizing and Framing for Commercial Buildings

We manufacture screens to any size – from small transom windows to massive loading dock doors (up to 4m wide for folding screens, 3m for sliding). Our frames are made from 6063‑T5 aluminum, powder‑coated in your choice of RAL colour. We also offer stainless steel frames for harsh environments. For arched or shaped openings, we can create custom frames to match.

Installation – Professional Recommended

For commercial projects, we strongly recommend professional installation to ensure proper fit, alignment, and safety. We can refer local contractors in most regions. For businesses with in‑house maintenance staff, DIY installation is possible with our detailed instructions. Bulk orders may qualify for on‑site installation training.

Compliance with Health and Safety Codes

MBORMC screens are designed to meet international health standards for insect exclusion. Our stainless steel mesh is easy to clean and does not absorb moisture or bacteria. Aluminum frames are non‑corrosive and can be sanitized with regular cleaning products. We can provide documentation for health inspections.
