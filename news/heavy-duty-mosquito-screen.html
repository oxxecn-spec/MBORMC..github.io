Heavy Duty Mosquito Screen – Industrial‑Strength Protection for High‑Traffic & Commercial Use

Not all mosquito screens are created equal. In high‑traffic areas – commercial kitchens, warehouse loading docks, busy patios, or homes with large dogs – standard screens can fail quickly. A heavy duty mosquito screen is built to withstand constant use, impact, and environmental stress. MBORMC manufactures heavy duty screens using 304 stainless steel mesh, reinforced aluminum frames, and commercial‑grade hardware. In this guide, we’ll explain the features of heavy duty screens, where they are needed, and how to order a custom solution for your business or home.

What Makes a Screen “Heavy Duty”?

A heavy duty mosquito screen goes beyond ordinary construction in several ways:

  • Mesh material: 304 stainless steel (rather than PET/fiberglass) for tear and scratch resistance.
  • Frame profile: Thicker aluminum (30mm × 15mm or larger) with reinforced corners.
  • Hardware: Stainless steel screws, heavy‑duty springs (for retractable screens), and robust tracks.
  • Installation method: Screw‑mount or bolt‑through for maximum security.

Applications for Heavy Duty Screens

  • Commercial kitchens and restaurants: Where doors are opened hundreds of times per day and health inspectors demand insect exclusion.
  • Warehouse and factory loading docks: Screens that can withstand forklift traffic and accidental impacts.
  • Hospitality (hotels, resorts): High‑traffic patio doors and balcony entrances.
  • Schools and daycare centers: Durable screens that resist rough handling by children.
  • Homes with multiple large dogs: The ultimate pet‑proof solution.
  • Coastal properties: Resistance to salt spray and high winds.

Heavy Duty Mesh: 304 Stainless Steel Options

We offer two densities of 304 stainless steel mesh for heavy duty screens:

  • 18×16 mesh: Blocks mosquitoes, flies, wasps. Adequate for most commercial applications.
  • 20×20 mesh: Finer mesh, blocks no‑see‑ums and tiny midges. Ideal for tropical areas.

Both are available in dark grey or black. The mesh is woven from wire that is thicker (0.3mm‑0.4mm) than standard PET threads, giving it exceptional strength.

Reinforced Frame Options

For heavy duty applications, we recommend:

  • Heavy‑duty fixed frame (30mm × 15mm): Corner keys are reinforced with additional screws.
  • Commercial sliding screen frame (40mm × 20mm): For large sliding doors, with extra‑thick track.
  • Industrial hinged frame: For doors that swing, with heavy‑duty hinges and magnetic latches.

All frames are powder‑coated in your choice of colour.

Screen Styles for Heavy Duty Use

  • Fixed frame screens: Most durable – no moving parts. Ideal for windows and less‑frequented doors.
  • Sliding screens: Heavy‑duty sliding screen doors with reinforced rollers and tracks.
  • Folding (pleated) screens: For wide openings, but note that the folding mechanism may be a point of wear; we recommend sliding or fixed for extreme duty.
  • Retractable screens: Available with stainless steel mesh and heavy‑duty springs, but fixed or sliding are more robust for commercial use.

Installation Considerations for Heavy Duty Screens

Heavy duty screens are heavier than standard screens. Installation requires:

  • Use of power tools (drill, impact driver).
  • Wall anchors for masonry or concrete.
  • Two people for large screens.

We provide detailed instructions, but for commercial installations, you may prefer to hire a professional installer. We can recommend local contractors.

Custom Sizing for Non‑Standard Openings

We manufacture heavy duty screens to your exact dimensions. Maximum size for a single fixed frame: 3m × 3m (larger requires multi‑panel construction). For sliding screens, max width 2m, max height 3m. Provide width and height in cm or inches, and note any special requirements (e.g., custom shape, cutout for a handle).

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Are heavy duty screens also pet proof?
A: Yes, 304 stainless steel mesh is the same as our pet‑proof screen. Heavy duty frames add extra durability.

Q: Can I use a heavy duty screen on a fire escape or emergency exit?
A: Yes, we can design screens that are easily removable from the inside without tools, meeting safety codes.

Q: How do I clean a heavy duty stainless steel screen?
A: Vacuum with a brush attachment or pressure wash (low pressure). Stainless steel can also be wiped with a damp cloth.

Q: What warranty do you offer on heavy duty screens?
A: 2 years on manufacturing defects. Stainless steel mesh has a 10‑year lifespan under normal use.
