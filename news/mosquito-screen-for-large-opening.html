Mosquito Screen for Large Opening – Custom Solutions for Wide Doors, Patios & Commercial Spaces

Published on May 7, 2026 | By MBORMC Team
Large Opening Screen Pleated Screen Door Retractable Screen Sliding Screen Commercial Screen

Standard mosquito screens are designed for typical windows and doors, but what about large openings – wide sliding doors, French doors, patio entrances, or commercial storefronts? A mosquito screen for large opening requires specialized engineering: wider spans, heavier materials, and sometimes motorized operation. MBORMC manufactures custom screens for openings up to 4 meters wide (single panel) or even wider using multi‑panel systems. In this guide, we’ll explore the best screen types for large openings, how to ensure smooth operation despite the size, and installation considerations.

What Qualifies as a “Large Opening”?

For screening purposes, a “large opening” typically exceeds 1.5 meters in width or 2.5 meters in height. Standard retractable screens max out around 1.5m wide because wider cassettes become heavy and prone to sagging. For larger spans, alternative technologies are needed: pleated (folding) screens, heavy‑duty sliding screens, or bi‑parting systems. Common large openings include:

  • Wide sliding glass doors (2‑3 panels).
  • French doors (double doors, often 1.8‑2.4m total width).
  • Restaurant patio openings (floor‑to‑ceiling glass walls).
  • Garage doors (human entry doors – not vehicle doors).
  • Commercial storefronts and showroom entrances.

Best Screen Types for Large Openings

  • Folding (Pleated) Screens – The most versatile solution for wide openings. The mesh folds like an accordion and stacks at one or both sides. Available in single‑stack or bi‑parting (center‑meeting). Folding screens can handle widths up to 4 meters in a single unit, and up to 8 meters with two units. They glide on top and bottom tracks, requiring minimal side space.
  • Heavy‑Duty Sliding Screens – For sliding glass doors, a reinforced sliding screen with industrial‑grade rollers can be made up to 3 meters wide. The frame is extra‑thick (40mm × 20mm) and the mesh is always stainless steel for rigidity.
  • Bi‑Parting Retractable Screens – Two retractable screens that meet in the middle. Each cassette covers half the width. This allows for very wide openings (up to 6 meters total) while keeping cassette sizes manageable. However, there will be a visible seam where the two screens meet.
  • Motorized Screens – For openings over 3 meters, motorized folding or retractable screens are recommended. A motor provides consistent tension and smooth operation, eliminating the need for heavy pulling. Remote or wall‑switch control adds convenience.

Mesh Material for Large Openings

For large openings, 304 stainless steel mesh is strongly recommended. PET mesh may sag over time on wide spans. Stainless steel is also more resistant to damage from accidental impacts. Mesh density: 18×16 for most insects, or 20×20 for no‑see‑ums. Dark grey or black mesh is preferred – it reduces glare and is less noticeable against the large opening.

Frame and Track Requirements

Large screens require robust framing. We use:

  • Top rail: 30mm × 30mm aluminum with reinforced corners.
  • Bottom track: 20mm high with a low‑profile design to avoid tripping hazards.
  • For folding screens, the rail includes a guide channel for the pleated mesh.
  • For sliding screens, the track is equipped with stainless steel ball‑bearing rollers.

All frames are powder‑coated to match your existing trim.

How to Measure for a Large Opening Screen

  • Measure the width and height of the opening at multiple points (left, center, right; top, middle, bottom). Use the smallest measurements.
  • For folding screens, note if you prefer the stack on the left, right, or both sides (bi‑parting).
  • For sliding doors, measure the fixed panel width (the side that does not slide). If you want a screen that covers the entire opening (both fixed and moving panels), you need a folding or bi‑parting system.
  • Provide photos of the opening, including any obstructions (handles, lock, floor threshold).

DIY vs. Professional Installation

Installing a screen for a large opening is more complex than a standard window. We recommend professional installation for spans over 2.5m. Our installation partners have experience with large‑format screens. For DIY enthusiasts, we provide detailed instructions and pre‑assembled components, but you will need at least two people to handle the weight of a 3‑4m wide screen. Basic tools include a drill, level, and screwdrivers.

Motorization and Smart Features

For large openings, motorized operation offers several benefits:

  • Effortless opening/closing – no heavy pulling.
  • Can be integrated with home automation systems (Wi‑Fi, Zigbee, Z‑Wave).
  • Can be scheduled to close automatically at dusk when mosquitoes are active.
  • Ideal for commercial applications where the screen is operated frequently.

We offer both 110V and 220V motor options, with battery backup for power outages.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the maximum width for a single folding screen?
A: We can make folding screens up to 4 meters wide in a single unit. For wider openings, we use two units that meet in the middle (bi‑parting).

Q: Will a wide screen sag over time?
A: With stainless steel mesh and proper tensioning, sagging is minimal. Motorized screens maintain constant tension. We also offer a tension adjustment mechanism for folding screens.

Q: Can I install a screen on an opening that has no top beam?
A: Yes, but we may need to design a freestanding frame or use side‑mounted tracks. Contact us for custom engineering.

Q: Are large screens suitable for high‑wind areas?
A: Yes, with reinforced frames and wind‑rated mesh. We can provide engineering data for your location.
