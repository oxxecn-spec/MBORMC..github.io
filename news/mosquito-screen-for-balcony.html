Why Your Balcony Needs a Mosquito Screen

Balconies are prime entry points for insects. Open balcony doors invite mosquitoes, flies, and even wasps into your living space. A screen lets you enjoy the breeze and outdoor access without the pests. For apartment dwellers, a balcony screen is often the only way to safely ventilate without inviting bugs. It also keeps out dust, pollen, and leaves, keeping your balcony cleaner. In high‑rise buildings, wind can blow insects up – a screen stops them at the door.

Best Screen Types for Balconies

The ideal balcony screen depends on your balcony layout and how you use the door.

  • Retractable Roll‑Up Screens – Most popular for balcony doors. The screen rolls into a slim cassette at the side. When not needed, it disappears completely. Perfect for sliding or hinged balcony doors.
  • Sliding Screens – For sliding balcony doors, a sliding screen on a track is a classic solution. It slides open with the door and is very durable.
  • Magnetic Curtain Screens – For renters who cannot drill, a magnetic screen attaches to the door frame with tape. No tools required. Split magnets allow hands‑free passage.
  • Pleated Folding Screens – For wide balcony openings (e.g., floor‑to‑ceiling glass walls), a folding screen that stacks at the side works well.

Mesh Material for Balcony Screens

We recommend 304 stainless steel mesh for balcony screens if you have pets or if the balcony is exposed to strong wind (which can flap PET mesh). For most balconies, PET fiber mesh is sufficient – it’s lightweight, UV‑resistant, and offers excellent visibility. Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black. Dark mesh reduces glare and is less noticeable from outside.

Measurement Tips for Balcony Doors

  • Measure the width and height of the door opening (inside the frame).
  • For sliding doors, also note whether the door slides left or right – the screen cassette should mount on the stationary side.
  • For hinged doors, measure the clear opening when the door is open.
  • If your balcony has a high threshold, choose a screen with a bottom track or a weighted magnetic curtain.

DIY Installation – Simple Process

Installing a balcony screen is straightforward. For retractable screens, mount the cassette using screws (provided). For sliding screens, install the track and slide the panel in. For magnetic curtains, just clean the frame and apply the adhesive magnetic tape – takes 5 minutes. All hardware is included. Basic tools like a drill and screwdriver may be needed for some types.

Rental‑Friendly Options

If you rent your apartment, choose a magnetic screen or a Velcro‑attached screen. These require no drilling and can be removed without leaving marks. They are also much cheaper than permanent screens. However, they are less durable – expect 2‑3 years of use. For a balance of durability and no‑drill, consider a spring‑tensioned screen that pressure‑fits into the frame.
