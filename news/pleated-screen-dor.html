Pleated Screen Door – Space‑Saving, Accordion‑Style Protection for Any Door

Published on May 2, 2026 | By MBORMC Team

Doors are the primary entry points for insects, but traditional fixed screen doors can be bulky and visually intrusive. A pleated screen door (also called a concertina or folding screen door) offers a sleek, space‑saving alternative. The accordion‑fold mesh stacks compactly at the side when open, and expands to cover the entire doorway when closed. MBORMC manufactures custom pleated screen doors for French doors, sliding patio doors, single hinged doors, and even commercial entrances up to 4 meters wide. In this guide, we’ll explain how pleated screen doors work, their advantages, and how to install one yourself.

What Is a Pleated Screen Door?

A pleated screen door consists of a mesh that is folded like a fan (pleated). The folds allow the screen to be very compact when collapsed (typically 5–10 cm wide) and expand horizontally to cover the opening. The screen rides on a top rail and bottom track (or a tension wire for trackless installations). A handle allows you to slide it open or closed. When not in use, the screen stacks at one side (or both sides for bi‑parting systems). Unlike retractable screens, there is no cassette – just a clean stack of pleats.

Advantages of Pleated Screen Doors

  • Extra‑wide openings: Can cover widths up to 4 meters (13 feet) in a single unit – ideal for double French doors.
  • Slim profile: The folded stack is less bulky than a retractable cassette.
  • Smooth operation: Glides along a track with minimal effort.
  • Bi‑parting options: Two screens that meet in the middle for symmetrical operation.
  • No spring mechanism: Less to break compared to retractable screens.

Types of Pleated Screen Doors from MBORMC

  • Single‑pleat (one‑way): Stacks on the left or right side. For single doors or where the opening is used from one side.
  • Bi‑parting (center‑meeting): Two screens that stack on both sides and meet in the middle. Perfect for French doors where both doors open outward.
  • Top‑mount only (no bottom track): For doorways with a high threshold – the screen uses a weighted bottom or tension wire.
  • With integrated handle and latch: Our standard pleated screens include a handle for pulling and a magnetic or mechanical latch to hold the screen closed.

Mesh Material Options

We offer pleated screen doors with PET fiber mesh (lightweight, UV‑resistant, 18×16 density) – standard for most homes. For high‑traffic areas or homes with pets, we strongly recommend 304 stainless steel mesh. Stainless steel pleated mesh is more durable and resists tearing from claws. Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black. Darker mesh reduces glare and visibility from outside.

DIY Installation – Step by Step

Installing a pleated screen door is a straightforward DIY project. Here’s the process:

  • Measure the width and height of your door opening. For French doors, measure each leaf individually.
  • Order the custom pleated screen door from MBORMC with your exact dimensions, stacking direction (left, right, or bi‑parting), and track preference.
  • Install the top rail using screws (wall anchors provided). Use a level to ensure it’s straight.
  • If using a bottom track, install it aligned with the top rail.
  • Attach the screen assembly to the rail – it clips into place.
  • Install the handle and latch on the moving edge.
  • Test sliding – adjust the tension if needed (screws on the track).

Basic tools required: drill, screwdriver, level, measuring tape. Total time: 30–60 minutes.

Where to Use Pleated Screen Doors

  • French doors: The most popular application – one screen per leaf or a bi‑parting system.
  • Sliding patio doors: Can be mounted on the interior side, sliding horizontally.
  • Single hinged entry doors: Mount on the inside, slides to the side.
  • Restaurant patios: Commercial‑grade pleated screens for wide openings.
  • Garage doors (human entrance): Side door can be fitted with a pleated screen.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I install a pleated screen door on a door that opens outward?
A: Yes. The screen must be mounted on the interior side. Ensure the door swing does not hit the screen when open. Measure the clearance.

Q: How durable is the pleated mesh?
A: PET pleated mesh lasts 5–8 years with normal use. Stainless steel lasts 10–20 years. The pleats may wear over time, but replacement mesh can be ordered.

Q: How do I clean a pleated screen door?
A: Vacuum with a brush attachment. For deeper cleaning, use a soft brush and mild soapy water. Do not machine wash.

Q: What is the difference between a pleated screen door and a retractable screen door?
A: Retractable screens roll into a cassette and disappear completely. Pleated screens fold into a stack that remains visible, but they can be much wider and are often simpler mechanically.
