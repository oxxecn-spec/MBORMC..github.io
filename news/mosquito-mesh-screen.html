Mosquito Mesh Screen – Choosing the Right Material & Density for Complete Protection

Published on May 1, 2026 | By MBORMC Team

The effectiveness of any insect screen comes down to one thing: the mesh. A mosquito mesh screen must have the right combination of aperture size, material strength, and transparency to block insects while allowing airflow and visibility. MBORMC offers two premium mesh materials – PET fiber and 304 stainless steel – in multiple densities (18×16, 20×20, and custom). In this guide, we’ll explain mesh counts, material properties, and how to choose the best mosquito mesh for your home or business.

What Is Mosquito Mesh?

Mosquito mesh is a woven fabric made from either synthetic fibers (PET/polyester) or metal wire (stainless steel). It is designed to be stretched across a frame to cover openings like windows and doors. The key specification is the “mesh count” – the number of threads per linear inch (or per square inch). A higher mesh count means smaller holes, which block smaller insects but may reduce airflow and visibility slightly. Standard mosquito mesh is 18×16, meaning 18 threads per inch horizontally and 16 vertically. For tiny mosquitoes like Aedes aegypti, 20×20 or finer is recommended.

Mesh Density: 18×16 vs. 20×20 vs. Custom

  • 18×16 mesh (standard): Hole size approx. 1.2mm – blocks house flies, most mosquitoes, and large midges. Suitable for general residential use in areas without high dengue risk. Airflow: ~85% openness.
  • 20×20 mesh (anti‑mosquito grade): Hole size approx. 0.6mm – blocks Aedes aegypti, Anopheles, Culex, and most biting midges. Recommended for tropical regions and disease‑endemic areas. Airflow: ~75% openness.
  • 24×24 mesh (ultra‑fine): Hole size approx. 0.4mm – blocks even the smallest sand flies (no‑see‑ums) and thrips. Airflow: ~65% openness. Available on request for severe infestations.

MBORMC’s standard offering is 18×16 PET or stainless steel. Upgrade to 20×20 for enhanced mosquito protection. For specialized needs (e.g., near marshlands with sand flies), we can source 24×24 mesh.

Material Comparison: PET Fiber vs. 304 Stainless Steel

PET Fiber Mesh (polyester):

  • Lightweight, flexible, and easy to handle.
  • UV‑stabilized – resists sun damage for 5–10 years.
  • Rust‑proof, suitable for humid coastal areas.
  • Color options: grey, dark grey, black (dark colors reduce glare and improve outward visibility).
  • Transparency: >85% for 18×16, ~80% for 20×20.
  • Cost: Affordable.

304 Stainless Steel Mesh:

  • Heavy‑duty, tear‑resistant, and pet‑proof (resists scratching/biting).
  • Fire‑resistant and extremely durable (10–20+ years).
  • Corrosion‑resistant (304 grade is suitable for coastal areas).
  • Color: typically dark grey or black (powder‑coated).
  • Transparency: slightly lower than PET (about 75% for 18×16, 70% for 20×20).
  • Cost: Higher than PET, but lasts much longer.

Choose PET for budget‑friendly, lightweight solutions. Choose stainless steel for high‑traffic areas, homes with pets, or where maximum durability is required.

Frame Types and Installation

MBORMC offers several frame styles compatible with mosquito mesh:

  • Fixed aluminum frames: Permanent installation using screws or clips. Best for windows that are rarely opened. Provides the tightest seal.
  • Retractable cassettes: The mesh rolls into a slim box at the side or top. Ideal for doors and windows where you want the screen to disappear when not in use.
  • Folding concertina frames: The mesh folds like an accordion. Great for wide openings.
  • Velcro‑stick frames: No drilling – the mesh attaches to Velcro tape on the window frame. Perfect for renters or temporary use.
  • Magnetic frames: The mesh attaches to magnetic strips. Easy to remove for cleaning.

DIY Installation – Simple Steps

Installing a mosquito mesh screen is straightforward. For Velcro screens: clean the window frame, apply the tape, then press the screen onto it. For fixed frames: measure your opening, order the custom‑size screen, then attach the frame using the provided screws or clips. For retractable screens: mount the cassette, run the guide rails, and snap the screen into place. All our screens come with detailed instructions. Basic tools like a screwdriver or drill may be needed for some types, but no professional skills are required.

Frequently Asked Questions About Mosquito Mesh Screens

Q: Can I replace the mesh on my existing screen frame?
A: Yes, we can supply just the mesh (with spline) for you to re‑screen your own frame. Contact us with your frame dimensions and mesh type.

Q: Does the mesh color affect mosquito attraction?
A: Mosquitoes are attracted to light colors. Dark grey or black mesh is less attractive to mosquitoes and also provides better outward visibility.

Q: How often should I replace PET mesh?
A: PET lasts 5–10 years depending on sun exposure. Stainless steel can last 10–20 years.

Q: Will a 20×20 mesh block pollen and dust?
A: Yes. The finer mesh will trap more airborne particles, improving indoor air quality.
