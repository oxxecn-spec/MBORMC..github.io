Roll Up Mosquito Screen – Compact Spring-Loaded Protection

A roll up mosquito screen (also called a retractable roller screen) offers the ultimate convenience: insect protection when you need it, and a completely clear opening when you don’t. The mesh is spring‑loaded and rolls up into a compact cassette, disappearing from sight. MBORMC manufactures custom roll up screens for windows, doors, and even large patio openings. They are self‑retracting, meaning you simply pull the screen down (or across) and it locks into place; release the latch and it rolls back up automatically.

What is a Roll Up Mosquito Screen?

A roll up mosquito screen consists of a mesh that is attached to a spring‑loaded roller inside a cassette. The cassette can be mounted at the top (for vertical pull‑down) or at one side (for horizontal pull‑across). When you pull the handle, the mesh unrolls and a latch holds it in place at the opposite side. When you release the latch, the spring gently rolls the mesh back into the cassette. The cassette is typically 30–50mm in diameter and can be colour‑matched to your window or door frame, making it virtually invisible when retracted.

Advantages of Roll Up Screens

  • No obstruction when not in use: The screen completely disappears into the cassette.
  • Self‑retracting: No need to manually fold or stack – springs do the work.
  • Clean aesthetic: The cassette blends with the frame.
  • Ideal for windows that are opened only occasionally.
  • Works on both hinged and sliding windows.

Types of Roll Up Screens from MBORMC

  • Vertical pull‑down (top‑mount): Cassette mounted above the opening, pulls down. Common for single windows and French doors.
  • Horizontal pull‑across (side‑mount): Cassette mounted at the side, pulls across. Ideal for sliding doors and wide windows.
  • Bi‑parting (two cassettes): Two screens that meet in the middle for extra‑wide openings.
  • Magnetic latch version: For sliding doors, the screen can be equipped with a magnetic catch that snaps shut automatically.

Mesh Material Options

We offer roll up screens with PET fiber mesh (lightweight, UV‑resistant, 18×16 density) – standard for windows. For doors or high‑traffic areas, we recommend 304 stainless steel mesh which is stronger and pet‑proof. Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black. Darker mesh offers better outward visibility and reduces glare.

DIY Installation – Step by Step

Installing a roll up screen is a straightforward DIY project:

  • Measure the width and height of your opening.
  • Order the custom screen from MBORMC with your exact dimensions.
  • Mount the cassette using screws (wall anchors provided) – ensure it’s level.
  • For vertical pull‑down, attach the guide rails on both sides. For horizontal, attach top and bottom tracks or a single guide rail on the opposite side.
  • Clip the mesh edge into the pull handle and attach the latch at the opposite side.
  • Test the operation – the screen should roll up and down smoothly.

Basic tools: drill, screwdriver, level. Time: 20–40 minutes.

Custom Sizing – Perfect Fit

We manufacture each roll up screen to your exact measurements. Maximum width for a single cassette is 1.5m (for smooth spring operation). For wider openings, use a bi‑parting system. Maximum height is 2.5m for vertical pull‑down screens. Provide width and height in cm or inches.

Where to Use Roll Up Screens

  • Kitchen windows: Roll up when you need fresh air, retract when you want a clear view.
  • Bedroom windows: Pull down at night for mosquito protection, roll up during the day.
  • French doors: Use two vertical cassettes (one per door).
  • RV and camper van windows: Compact and easy to install in small spaces.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does the spring mechanism last?
A: Our springs are rated for over 10,000 cycles. That’s daily use for over 27 years.

Q: Can I install a roll up screen on a window that opens outward?
A: Yes. Use a side‑mount horizontal cassette so the screen pulls across, not down into the swing path.

Q: How do I clean the mesh?
A: Vacuum with a brush attachment. The mesh can also be unrolled and wiped with a damp cloth.
