Why Your Patio Door Needs a Mosquito Screen

Patio doors are opened frequently, especially during warm weather. Without a screen, each opening is an invitation to mosquitoes, flies, wasps, and moths. A screen lets you keep the door open for ventilation, easy access to barbecues and gardens, and to let pets in and out. It also helps keep out dust, pollen, and leaves. For homes with children, a screened patio door allows safe indoor‑outdoor play. In many regions, screening is essential to prevent mosquito‑borne diseases.

Best Screen Types for Patio Doors

The ideal screen depends on your door style and how you use it.

  • Sliding Screen Door – The classic solution for sliding glass patio doors. A rigid frame screen slides on a track parallel to the door. It’s durable, easy to operate, and can be made with pet‑proof stainless steel mesh.
  • Retractable Roll‑Up Screen – For a clean, invisible look, a retractable screen rolls into a slim cassette at the side. When not needed, the screen disappears completely. Perfect for sliding or hinged patio doors.
  • Folding (Pleated) Screen – Ideal for French patio doors (double hinged). A bi‑parting screen stacks on both sides and meets in the middle. Allows each door to open independently.
  • Magnetic Curtain Screen – For renters or temporary use, a magnetic screen attaches to the door frame with tape. No drilling. Walk through and it snaps shut.

Mesh Material for Patio Doors

For most patio doors, PET fiber mesh (18×16) is sufficient – it’s lightweight, UV‑resistant, and offers excellent visibility. However, if you have pets that scratch or live in a high‑traffic area, we strongly recommend 304 stainless steel mesh. Stainless steel resists tearing and lasts 10‑20 years. Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black. Darker mesh reduces glare and is less visible from outside.

Measurement Tips for Patio Doors

  • For sliding doors: measure the width and height of the fixed panel opening (the side that doesn’t slide). The screen will cover that opening. Also note which side the door slides.
  • For French doors: measure the overall width of both doors when closed, and the height. For a bi‑parting screen, we’ll make two screens that meet in the middle.
  • For single hinged doors: measure the clear door opening (inside the frame).

DIY Installation – Simple Process

Installing a patio door screen is a weekend DIY project. For sliding screens, install the top and bottom tracks (screw‑in or adhesive), then slide the screen panel into place. For retractable screens, mount the cassette using screws, attach the guide rails, and snap the screen in. For magnetic screens, just clean the frame and apply the adhesive magnetic tape. All hardware is included. Basic tools (drill, screwdriver, level) may be needed.

Pet‑Friendly and Child‑Safe Options

If you have dogs or children, choose a heavy‑duty stainless steel mesh with a reinforced frame. Our screens can also be equipped with a pet flap (small door) for dogs to pass through without damaging the screen. For child safety, retractable screens have no hanging cords – they use a spring mechanism that is safe for children.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I install a mosquito screen on a patio door that opens outward?
A: Yes. For outward‑opening doors, the screen must be mounted on the interior side. Retractable or sliding screens work well.

Q: Will a sliding screen door affect my door’s operation?
A: No. The screen slides independently on its own track. You can open the glass door while the screen is closed.

Q: How do I clean a patio door screen?
A: Vacuum with a brush attachment. Sliding screens can be lifted out and rinsed with mild soap and water.

Q: What is the maximum size for a sliding screen door?
A: We can make sliding screens up to 3m wide (single panel) and 3m high. For larger openings, consider bi‑parting sliding screens or folding screens.
