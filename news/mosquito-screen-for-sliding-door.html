Why Your Sliding Door Needs a Mosquito Screen

Sliding doors are often left open during warm weather to create a seamless indoor‑outdoor flow. But without a screen, mosquitoes, flies, wasps, and even larger insects can enter freely. A screen lets you enjoy the breeze, reduce air conditioning use, and let pets in and out. It also keeps out dust, pollen, and small debris. For homes near gardens, pools, or wooded areas, a sliding door screen is essential for comfort and health.

Best Screen Types for Sliding Doors

There are three main types of screens for sliding doors, each with its own advantages:

  • Traditional Sliding Screen Door – A rigid frame screen that slides on its own track parallel to the glass door. It’s durable, easy to operate, and can be made with 304 stainless steel mesh for pet‑proofing. This is the most common solution and offers the best durability.
  • Retractable Roll‑Up Screen – The screen rolls into a slim cassette mounted at the side. When not needed, it disappears completely, leaving your view unobstructed. Ideal for those who want a clean, modern look.
  • Pleated Folding Screen – A concertina‑style screen that folds to one side. Suitable for very wide sliding doors (over 2.5m) where a single rigid panel would be too heavy.

Mesh Material Options

For sliding doors, we offer two mesh choices:

  • PET fiber mesh (18×16) – Lightweight, affordable, and offers excellent visibility (85% openness). UV‑resistant and rust‑proof. Ideal for most residential sliding doors.
  • 304 stainless steel mesh – Heavy‑duty, pet‑proof, and extremely durable (10‑20 years). Recommended if you have dogs that scratch or if the door is in a high‑traffic area.

Both are available in grey, dark grey, or black. Darker mesh reduces glare and is less noticeable from outside.

How to Measure for a Sliding Door Screen

  • Measure the width of the fixed panel opening (the side that does not slide). The screen will cover this opening.
  • Measure the height from the top of the track to the floor (or to the bottom track).
  • If you are replacing an existing sliding screen door, measure the outside dimensions of the old screen frame.
  • For retractable screens, measure the full opening width – we will make a cassette that covers the entire opening.

DIY Installation – Simple Steps

Installing a sliding door screen is a weekend DIY project. For traditional sliding screens:

  1. Install the top and bottom tracks using screws (provided). Use a level to ensure they are straight and aligned.
  2. Slide the screen panel into the top track first, then lower it into the bottom track.
  3. Adjust the roller tension screws to ensure smooth sliding.
  4. Install the handle on the moving edge.

For retractable screens: mount the cassette, attach the guide rails, and snap the screen in place. All hardware is included. Basic tools (drill, screwdriver, level) are required.

Pet‑Friendly and Child‑Safe Features

If you have dogs or children, choose a 304 stainless steel mesh with a reinforced aluminum frame. The metal mesh resists scratching and pushing. For homes with multiple pets, we also offer a pet flap door cut into the screen, allowing pets to pass through without damaging the screen. Retractable screens have no hanging cords, making them safe for children.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I install a mosquito screen on a sliding door that already has a screen?
A: Yes, you can replace an old or damaged screen. Remove the old screen and order a custom replacement from MBORMC.

Q: Will a sliding screen door affect the operation of my glass door?
A: No. The screen slides on its own independent track. You can open the glass door while the screen remains closed.

Q: How do I clean a sliding screen door?
A: Vacuum the mesh with a brush attachment. For deeper cleaning, lift the screen out of the track and rinse with mild soap and water.

Q: What is the maximum size for a sliding screen door?
A: We can manufacture single‑panel sliding screens up to 3m wide and 3m high. For larger openings, consider a bi‑parting sliding screen (two panels that meet in the middle) or a retractable screen.
