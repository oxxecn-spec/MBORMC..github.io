Mosquito Screen for Terrace – Enjoy Your Outdoor Living Space Bug‑Free

Published on May 5, 2026 | By MBORMC Team
Terrace Mosquito Screen Patio Door Screen Balcony Screen Retractable Screen Folding Screen

A terrace is a wonderful extension of your living space – ideal for dining, relaxing, or entertaining. But nothing ruins a pleasant evening faster than mosquitoes and flies. A mosquito screen for terrace allows you to enclose your terrace partially or fully, creating a bug‑free zone while preserving the open‑air feel. MBORMC offers custom screens for terrace openings of all sizes – from small doorways to large panoramic openings. In this guide, we’ll explore the best screen solutions for terraces, including retractable, sliding, and folding systems, and how to choose the right one for your space.

Why Your Terrace Needs an Insect Screen

Terraces are often left open to the elements, which means they are also open to insects. Even if you only step out for a few minutes, mosquitoes can sneak in. A screen allows you to keep your terrace door open for ventilation without worry. For larger terraces, you can create a screened enclosure that lets you enjoy the outdoors 24/7. Benefits include:

  • Uninterrupted enjoyment: No more swatting or spraying repellent.
  • Health protection: Reduces risk of mosquito‑borne diseases.
  • Pet and child safety: Keeps pets from wandering off and prevents insects from biting children.
  • Cleaner space: Keeps out leaves, dust, and pollen.

Best Screen Types for Terraces

The ideal screen depends on the size of your terrace opening and how you use the space.

  • Retractable Roll‑Up Screens – Perfect for terrace doors that are opened frequently. The screen rolls into a slim cassette at the side. When not needed, it disappears completely. Available for single doors or wide openings (up to 3m).
  • Folding (Pleated) Screens – Ideal for very wide terrace openings (up to 4m). The accordion‑fold mesh stacks at one or both sides. Can be configured as bi‑parting for double doors.
  • Sliding Screen Doors – For sliding glass terrace doors, a traditional sliding screen is durable and classic. It slides on a track parallel to the glass door.
  • Fixed Frame Screens – For large fixed openings (e.g., a screened porch), we can supply rigid panels with aluminum frames and stainless steel mesh. These are permanent installations.
  • Magnetic Curtain Screens – For renters or budget‑conscious, a magnetic screen attaches with tape. Not as durable for large openings but effective for temporary use.

Enclosing an Entire Terrace

If you want to turn your terrace into a fully screened “sunroom,” MBORMC can manufacture custom fixed screen panels that attach to your terrace’s architectural supports. We supply the frames and mesh; you or your contractor install them. This creates a permanent bug‑free outdoor room. We also offer sliding or folding screen walls for partial enclosure, allowing you to open sections when desired.

Mesh Material for Terrace Screens

For terrace screens, we recommend 304 stainless steel mesh for its durability, especially if the screen will be exposed to wind, rain, or pets. Stainless steel resists tearing and lasts 10‑20 years. For covered terraces or light use, PET fiber mesh is sufficient – it’s lighter and offers better visibility (85% openness). Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black. Dark mesh reduces glare and is less visible from outside.

How to Measure for a Terrace Screen

  • For a door: measure the width and height of the door opening (inside the frame).
  • For a large opening (e.g., between columns): measure the width between supports and the height from floor to ceiling or beam.
  • For a bi‑parting screen covering a wide opening, provide the total width and height; we will calculate panel sizes.
  • If your terrace has an irregular shape, contact us for a custom solution.

DIY Installation – Manageable for Most Homeowners

Installing a terrace screen is a DIY‑friendly project. Retractable screens require mounting the cassette and guide rails – basic tools (drill, screwdriver, level). Folding screens require installing top and bottom tracks. Fixed panels require attaching frames to the structure. All hardware is included. If you’re uncomfortable with DIY, we can recommend local installers.

Weather Resistance and Durability

Terrace screens are exposed to sun, rain, and wind. Our aluminum frames are powder‑coated for UV and corrosion resistance. Stainless steel mesh is rust‑proof. For high‑wind areas, we recommend reinforced tracks and heavier frames. All screens are designed to withstand normal outdoor conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I install a screen on a terrace that has no roof?
A: Yes, as long as there is a top structure (beam, awning) to mount the rail or cassette. For open‑top terraces, consider a retractable screen that mounts to the door frame only.

Q: Will a screen block my view of the landscape?
A: Our high‑transparency PET mesh has over 85% openness; you’ll barely notice it. Stainless steel offers about 75% openness – still very clear.

Q: How do I clean a terrace screen?
A: Vacuum with a brush attachment or rinse with a garden hose (for stainless steel). Avoid using high‑pressure washers near the edges.

Q: Can I get motorized terrace screens?
A: Yes, we offer motorized retractable screens for large openings. Control with remote or wall switch. Contact us for details.
