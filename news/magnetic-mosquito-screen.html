For renters, temporary residents, or anyone who wants a no‑drill insect protection solution, a magnetic mosquito screen is the perfect choice. It uses powerful magnetic strips to attach securely to metal or steel door/window frames, or to a magnetic tape applied to non‑magnetic surfaces. No tools, no screws, no permanent modifications. MBORMC manufactures custom magnetic mosquito screens for doors and windows, with your choice of PET or stainless steel mesh. In this guide, we’ll explain how magnetic screens work, their advantages, and how to install them in minutes.

What Is a Magnetic Mosquito Screen?

A magnetic mosquito screen consists of a mesh panel with magnetic strips sewn or glued around the edges. The screen adheres to a magnetic receiving surface – either directly to a metal frame (steel door/window) or to a self‑adhesive magnetic tape that you stick onto non‑magnetic frames (aluminum, PVC, wood). The magnets hold the screen securely in place, yet it can be easily removed for cleaning or seasonal storage. There are also magnetic walk‑through curtains for doors, where the screen splits vertically with magnets that snap shut after you pass through.

Advantages of Magnetic Screens

  • No drilling or tools: Install in seconds – no landlord permission needed.
  • Removable and reusable: Take it down in winter or when you move out.
  • Ideal for metal frames: Sticks directly to steel doors/windows.
  • Low cost: One of the most affordable insect screen options.
  • Walk‑through option: Magnetic curtain screens for doors allow hands‑free passage.

Types of Magnetic Screens from MBORMC

  • Fixed magnetic window screen: A solid mesh panel that attaches directly to the window frame via magnets. Removed by pulling it off.
  • Walk‑through magnetic door screen (curtain style): A split screen that hangs in the doorway. The center split has magnets that seal behind you. No frame required – just stick the hook‑and‑loop tape or magnets at the top and sides.
  • Magnetic screen with frame: A lightweight frame with embedded magnets that clip onto the metal frame. More rigid than curtain style.

Mesh Material Options

We offer magnetic screens with PET fiber mesh (standard, lightweight, 18×16 density) – ideal for most residential applications. For higher durability or pet resistance, 304 stainless steel mesh is available, though it is heavier and may require stronger magnets. Both materials come in grey, dark grey, or black.

DIY Installation – No Tools Required

Installing a magnetic mosquito screen is incredibly easy. Here’s how:

  • For metal window/door frames: Simply place the magnetic screen against the frame – it will stick directly. Ensure the magnets align with the metal surface.
  • For non‑metal frames (aluminum, PVC, wood): First clean the frame, then apply the self‑adhesive magnetic tape (included) around the perimeter. Then attach the magnetic screen to the tape.
  • For walk‑through magnetic door curtains: Use the included hook‑and‑loop tape or push pins to hang the curtain at the top. The sides may also have magnets or weights. No tape needed if the door frame is metal.

Total installation time: less than 5 minutes.

We manufacture each magnetic screen to your exact window or door dimensions. You provide width and height (in cm or inches). We cut the mesh and sew/attach the magnetic strips accordingly. For walk‑through curtains, we also need the opening width and height to ensure proper coverage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Will the magnets damage my window frame?
A: No. The magnets are rubber‑coated or encased in PVC and will not scratch surfaces.

Q: How strong are the magnets?
A> Strong enough to hold the screen in place during normal use and wind, but you can still remove it by pulling. For very windy areas, we can provide extra‑strength magnets.

Q: Can I leave a magnetic screen up permanently?
A: Yes, but prolonged exposure to direct sunlight may weaken the adhesive tape on non‑metal frames. For permanent installation, consider a fixed frame screen.

Q: How do I clean a magnetic screen?
A: Remove it from the frame, rinse with mild soap and water, and air dry. Magnets will retain their strength.
