What Is an Insect Protection Screen?

An insect protection screen is a fine mesh barrier designed to exclude all common flying insects – mosquitoes, house flies, stable flies, cluster flies, wasps, hornets, gnats, midges, sand flies, and even small moths. Unlike species‑specific solutions, a comprehensive insect protection screen uses a mesh with a small enough aperture (typically 18×16 or 20×20 threads per inch) to block even the tiniest biting insects like sand flies (no‑see‑ums). The screen is mounted on a frame (aluminum, PVC, or flexible) that attaches to windows, doors, patios, or other openings.

Why You Need Total Insect Protection

Different regions face different insect threats. In tropical and subtropical areas, mosquitoes carry dengue, Zika, chikungunya, and malaria. In temperate zones, flies and wasps are common nuisances. Near lakes and rivers, midges (biting gnats) can make outdoor life miserable. A single type of screen may not block all of them. An insect protection screen with fine mesh provides universal defense. It’s especially important for:

  • Homes near water bodies (lakes, rivers, marshes) – high mosquito and midge pressure.
  • Restaurants and cafes with outdoor seating – to comply with health codes and keep flies off food.
  • Hospitals and clinics – where insect‑borne diseases must be prevented.
  • Schools and daycare centers – to protect children from bites and stings.
  • Hotels and resorts – to enhance guest comfort and reduce complaints.

Types of Insect Protection Screens from MBORMC

  • Retractable Roll‑Up Screens: Ideal for windows and doors where you want the screen to disappear when not needed. The mesh rolls into a compact cassette. Great for sliding doors and casement windows.
  • Folding Concertina Screens: Perfect for wide openings like French doors, large windows, or commercial entryways. Folds neatly to one or both sides.

Mesh Material: PET vs. 304 Stainless Steel

MBORMC uses two primary mesh materials, each with advantages:

  • PET Fiber Mesh: Lightweight, affordable, and breathable. Offers over 85% openness for clear visibility and airflow. UV‑stabilized, rust‑proof. Available in grey, dark grey, or black. Lifespan 5–10 years. Ideal for residential use where pet damage isn’t a concern.
  • 304 Stainless Steel Mesh: Heavy‑duty, pet‑proof, fire‑resistant, and extremely durable (10–20 years). Slightly lower transparency (about 75%) but offers superior strength. Perfect for ground‑floor windows, doors, and commercial applications where vandalism or high traffic is expected.

Both mesh types can be cut to any size and are available in multiple colors (grey, dark grey, black). We also offer custom RAL frame colours to match your existing window/door frames.

DIY Installation – No Professional Required

All MBORMC insect protection screens are designed for easy DIY installation. Each order includes a detailed instruction manual with pictures. Complexity varies by screen type:

  • Fixed frame screens: Screws or clips attach to the frame. Requires a screwdriver. Around 15 minutes per window.
  • Retractable screens: Mount the cassette using screws (wall anchors provided). Install the guide rails. Takes about 30 minutes per door/window.
  • Folding screens: Mount the top rail and bottom track. The screen folds from a side stack. Takes about 30–45 minutes.
  • Velcro or magnetic screens: No tools – simply clean the surface, apply tape or magnets, and press the screen on. Takes 5 minutes.

We provide all necessary hardware. If you can measure and use a drill or screwdriver, you can install any of our screens.

Frequently Asked Questions About Insect Protection Screens

Q: What mesh size do I need to block sand flies (no‑see‑ums)?
A: For sand flies and tiny biting midges, use 20×20 threads per inch or finer. Our standard 18×16 blocks most, but we can supply 20×20 upon request.

Q: Can I install a screen on a large commercial storefront?
A: Yes. We offer extra‑wide folding screens and can custom‑engineer solutions for storefronts, warehouse doors, and loading docks. Contact us.

Q: How do I clean an insect protection screen?
A: Vacuum with a brush attachment, or wipe with a damp cloth. For deep cleaning, remove the screen if it’s Velcro/magnetic; fixed screens can be cleaned in place.

Q: Do your screens block dust and pollen?
A: Yes. The fine mesh also filters out dust, pollen, and small debris, improving indoor air quality – beneficial for allergy sufferers.
