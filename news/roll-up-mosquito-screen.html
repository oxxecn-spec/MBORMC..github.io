Roll Up Mosquito Screen – Compact Retractable Protection for Windows & Doors

A roll up mosquito screen (also known as a retractable screen) offers the ultimate in convenience: insect protection when you need it, complete openness when you don’t. The screen rolls into a slim cassette at the top (for windows) or side (for doors), disappearing entirely when retracted. MBORMC manufactures custom roll up screens for windows, hinged doors, and even sliding doors. In this guide, we’ll explain how roll up screens work, their advantages, and how you can install one yourself in under an hour.

What Is a Roll Up Mosquito Screen?

A roll up mosquito screen consists of a mesh screen attached to a spring‑loaded roller inside a compact cassette. The cassette can be mounted horizontally (above a window or door) or vertically (at the side). When you pull the screen down (or across), the mesh extends and locks into place at the opposite side. A gentle tug releases the latch, and the spring automatically rolls the screen back into the cassette. The cassette is typically only 2–3 inches wide and can be colour‑matched to your frame.

Advantages of Roll Up Screens

  • Disappears when not in use: No permanent screen blocking your view.
  • Ideal for windows and doors: Perfect for casement, awning, and hinged doors.
  • Clean, modern look: The cassette is discreet and blends with the frame.
  • Easy operation: One‑handed pull and release.
  • No bottom track needed (for vertical roll‑down): The screen uses side guides; no floor obstruction.

Types of Roll Up Screens from MBORMC

  • Vertical roll‑down (top‑mount): For windows and hinged doors. The cassette mounts above the opening, and you pull the screen downward.
  • Horizontal roll‑across (side‑mount): For sliding doors or wide windows. The cassette mounts at the side, and you pull the screen across.
  • Bi‑parting roll‑up: Two cassettes on both sides, meeting in the middle. For very wide openings (up to 4 meters).

Mesh Material Options

We offer roll up screens with PET fiber mesh (standard, lightweight, UV‑resistant) or 304 stainless steel mesh (heavy‑duty, pet‑proof, longer lifespan). PET is sufficient for most residential windows; stainless steel is recommended for doors that see frequent use or homes with pets. Both materials come in grey, dark grey, or black.

DIY Installation – Step by Step

Installing a roll up screen is a DIY project. Here’s the process:

  • Measure your window or door opening width and height.
  • Order the custom screen from MBORMC with your exact dimensions, specifying the cassette orientation (top or side).
  • Mount the cassette using screws (wall anchors provided). Use a level.
  • Attach the side guide rails (provided). For vertical roll‑down, install the bottom latch receiver.
  • Insert the screen pull bar into the guides, and attach the handle.
  • Test the operation – it should roll smoothly and latch securely.

Basic tools required: drill, screwdriver, level, measuring tape. Total time: 30–45 minutes.

Custom Sizing – Perfect for Any Opening

We manufacture each roll up screen to your exact measurements. Maximum width for a single cassette: 1.5m (for smooth spring operation). For wider openings, use a bi‑parting system or a pleated screen. Maximum height: 2.5m.

Where to Use Roll Up Screens

  • Kitchen windows: Roll up when cooking, pull down to keep flies out while ventilating.
  • Casement windows: A roll‑down screen is ideal because it doesn’t interfere with the crank.
  • Hinged entry doors: A side‑mount roll‑across screen covers the door opening without a floor track.
  • Patio doors (sliding): A side‑mount roll‑across screen works well, but a sliding screen door may be more durable for daily use.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does the spring last?
A: Our springs are rated for over 10,000 cycles – decades of normal use.

Q: Can I install a roll up screen on a window that opens outward?
A: Yes. For outward‑opening windows, use a vertical roll‑down cassette mounted on the interior side. Ensure the screen does not obstruct the window swing.

Q: How do I clean the screen?
A: Vacuum with a brush attachment. For deep cleaning, you can unclip the screen from the pull bar and rinse with mild soap and water.

Q: Is the screen pet‑proof?
A: Standard PET mesh can be scratched. Upgrade to stainless steel mesh for pet‑resistant protection.
