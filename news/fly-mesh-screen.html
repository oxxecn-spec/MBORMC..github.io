Fly Mesh Screen – High-Visibility Barrier for Flies, Wasps & Bees

Published on May 1, 2026 | By MBORMC Team
Fly Mesh Screen Fly Screen Insect Screen Mosquito Screen Velcro Screen

Flies are not only annoying – they carry harmful bacteria and can contaminate food preparation areas. Wasps and bees can sting, posing a risk to allergic individuals. A fly mesh screen provides a durable, high-visibility barrier that keeps flies, wasps, hornets, and bees out while allowing fresh air and natural light to flow freely. MBORMC manufactures custom fly mesh screens for residential kitchens, restaurant patios, commercial food service, and any area where flying pests are a problem. In this guide, we’ll discuss the best mesh types for fly exclusion, frame options, and easy DIY installation.

What Makes a Fly Mesh Screen Different?

Fly mesh is typically coarser (lower mesh count) than mosquito mesh because flies are larger insects. A mesh with 16×14 or 18×16 threads per inch is sufficient to block house flies, stable flies, bottle flies, and most wasps. Using a finer mesh (20×20) would work but reduces airflow more than necessary. MBORMC offers both standard fly mesh (16×14 or 18×16) and upgraded mosquito-grade mesh if you also need protection against tiny insects. For most fly‑only applications, 16×14 PET or stainless steel mesh is ideal – it provides excellent transparency (over 85% openness) and is very easy to clean.

Why You Need a Fly Mesh Screen

Flies are attracted to food, garbage, and organic matter. In kitchens, dining areas, and patios, they can land on food and spread pathogens like Salmonella and E. coli. In commercial settings, health codes often require fly screens on windows and doors serving food prep areas. For homes, a fly screen lets you keep windows open during summer without inviting a swarm into your kitchen. Wasps and bees can also be a nuisance during late summer; a mesh screen prevents them from entering while allowing you to enjoy the breeze.

  • Fixed Frame Screens: Permanent screens attached to window or door frames. Most durable and provide a tight seal. Ideal for kitchen windows.
  • Retractable Roll‑Up Screens: The mesh rolls into a cassette when not needed. Great for doors leading to patios or gardens where you want a bug‑free zone when open.

  • Magnetic Mesh Curtains: Walk‑through screens for doorways with auto‑closing magnets. Excellent for restaurant patio entrances.

Material Choice: PET vs. 304 Stainless Steel for Flies

PET fiber mesh is lightweight, affordable, and offers near‑invisible transparency (over 85%). PET is UV‑stabilized, resists rust, and is easy to clean. Lifespan: 5–10 years. Ideal for most residential applications. 304 stainless steel mesh is much stronger, pet‑proof, and fire‑resistant. It can withstand accidental impact (e.g., a broom handle) without tearing. Stainless steel lasts 10–20 years and is the preferred choice for commercial kitchens and high‑traffic areas. Both materials are available in grey, dark grey, or black. Dark colors reduce glare and make the screen less noticeable from the outside.

Installation – DIY Friendly

Installing a fly mesh screen is straightforward. For Velcro screens: clean the window frame, apply the tape, then press the screen onto it – no tools needed. For fixed frames: measure your opening, order the custom‑size screen, then attach the frame using screws or clips. For retractable or folding screens: follow the included step‑by‑step instruction manual; a drill and screwdriver may be required. All hardware is provided. Most installations take 15–30 minutes per screen.

Frequently Asked Questions About Fly Mesh Screens

Q: Can I use a fly mesh screen to block bees and wasps?
A: Yes. 16×14 mesh blocks most wasps and bees. For smaller species (like small sweat bees), 18×16 is recommended.

Q: How do I clean a fly mesh screen?
A: Vacuum with a brush attachment, or wipe with a damp cloth. For Velcro screens, remove and rinse with mild soap and water.

Q: Can I install a fly mesh screen on a sliding glass door?
A: Yes. Use a retractable screen that mounts at the side and slides across the opening, or a folding screen that stacks at one end.

Q: Do you offer screens for commercial kitchen doors?
A: Yes. We provide heavy‑duty stainless steel screens for commercial kitchens, including self‑closing magnetic screens for swinging doors.
