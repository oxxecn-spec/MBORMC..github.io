Mosquito Mesh Door – High‑Density Mesh Barrier for Complete Doorway Protection

The mesh you choose for your door screen determines its effectiveness, durability, and visibility. A mosquito mesh door uses specially engineered mesh fabric (PET or stainless steel) with a precise aperture size to block even the smallest flying insects while allowing maximum airflow and light transmission. MBORMC offers a range of mosquito mesh doors in different styles – fixed, retractable, folding, sliding, and magnetic – all fitted with high‑density mesh. In this guide, we’ll focus on the mesh itself: types, densities, colours, and how to select the right mesh for your home.

What Makes Mosquito Mesh Different?

Mosquito mesh is not ordinary screen fabric. It is woven to specific thread counts per inch (e.g., 18×16, 20×20) that create holes small enough to exclude mosquitoes and other insects while remaining transparent enough not to obstruct the view. The best mosquito mesh also resists UV degradation, tearing, and corrosion. MBORMC uses only premium mesh from certified suppliers, ensuring consistent quality.

Mesh Density Options

  • 18×16 mesh (standard): Holes approx. 1.2mm – blocks house flies, most mosquitoes, and large midges. Suitable for general residential use in areas without high disease pressure. Airflow: ~85% openness.
  • 20×20 mesh (anti‑mosquito grade): Holes approx. 0.6mm – blocks Aedes aegypti (dengue mosquito), Anopheles, Culex, and most biting midges. Recommended for tropical regions and disease‑endemic areas. Airflow: ~75% openness.
  • 24×24 mesh (ultra‑fine): Holes approx. 0.4mm – blocks even the smallest sand flies (no‑see‑ums) and thrips. Available on request. Airflow: ~65% openness.

MBORMC’s standard offering is 18×16 PET or stainless steel. Upgrade to 20×20 for enhanced mosquito protection. For specialized needs (e.g., near marshlands with sand flies), we can source 24×24 mesh.

Mesh Material: PET vs. 304 Stainless Steel

PET fiber mesh (polyester):

  • Lightweight, flexible, and easy to handle.
  • UV‑stabilized – resists sun damage for 5–10 years.
  • Rust‑proof, suitable for humid coastal areas.
  • Color options: grey, dark grey, black (dark colors reduce glare and improve outward visibility).
  • Cost: Affordable.

304 stainless steel mesh:

  • Heavy‑duty, tear‑resistant, and pet‑proof (resists scratching/biting).
  • Fire‑resistant and extremely durable (10–20+ years).
  • Corrosion‑resistant (304 grade is suitable for coastal areas).
  • Color: typically dark grey or black (powder‑coated).
  • Transparency: slightly lower than PET (about 75% for 18×16, 70% for 20×20).
  • Cost: Higher than PET, but lasts much longer.

Mesh Colour and Visibility

Many people believe white mesh offers the best visibility, but the opposite is true. Dark grey or black mesh significantly reduces glare from the sun, making it easier to see outside. From the exterior, black mesh blends with window tint and is less noticeable. For this reason, MBORMC primarily offers grey, dark grey, and black mesh. We can supply charcoal grey mesh upon request.

Types of Mosquito Mesh Doors

The mesh itself can be incorporated into various door screen styles:

  • Fixed mesh door: The mesh is stretched over a rigid aluminum frame and mounted permanently. Most durable and provides the tightest seal.
  • Retractable mesh door: The mesh rolls into a cassette when not needed. The mesh is sewn onto a spring‑loaded roller.
  • Folding (pleated) mesh door: The mesh is pleated and folds like an accordion. Ideal for wide openings.
  • Sliding mesh door: The mesh is mounted on a sliding frame that runs on a track.
  • Magnetic mesh door: The mesh is attached to magnetic strips, allowing easy removal and no drilling.

How to Choose the Right Mesh for Your Door

  • For mosquito‑prone areas (e.g., dengue hotspots): Choose 20×20 stainless steel or PET mesh.
  • For general insect protection: 18×16 PET mesh is sufficient and more affordable.
  • If you have pets that scratch: Choose 304 stainless steel mesh (PET will tear).
  • For high‑traffic doors: Choose a retractable or folding screen with stainless steel mesh.
  • For renters: Choose a magnetic or Velcro screen with PET mesh.

We manufacture each mosquito mesh door to your exact doorway dimensions. You provide width and height (in cm or inches). We cut the mesh and frame precisely, leaving no gaps. DIY Installation

Installing a mosquito mesh door is straightforward. For fixed screens, place the frame into the opening and secure with screws or spring clips. For retractable and folding screens, follow the included instruction manual – basic tools (drill, screwdriver) are required. For magnetic screens, no tools are needed. All hardware is provided, and we offer video tutorials upon request.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I replace the mesh on my existing door screen?
A: Yes, we can supply just the mesh (with spline) for you to re‑screen your own frame. Contact us with your frame dimensions.

Q: Does mesh colour affect mosquito attraction?
A: Mosquitoes are attracted to light colors. Dark grey or black mesh is less attractive to mosquitoes and also provides better outward visibility.

Q: How often should I replace PET mesh?
A: PET lasts 5–10 years depending on sun exposure. Stainless steel can last 10–20 years.

Q: Will a 20×20 mesh block pollen and dust?
A: Yes. The finer mesh will trap more airborne particles, improving indoor air quality.
